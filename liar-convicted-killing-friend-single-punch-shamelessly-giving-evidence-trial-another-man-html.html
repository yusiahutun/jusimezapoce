A man who gave evidence in a major trial following the manslaughter of his best friend was today found guilty of the killing himself.

Adrian Cooksey, 50, died when he struck his head on the ground after he was felled by a single punch following a drinking session in March 2009.

Life-long friend Richard Elmes gave evidence during the trial of student Tom Minshull who was acquitted in November 2009 of Mr Cooksey's manslaughter.

Elmes today pleaded guilty at Bristol Crown Court for committing perjury during Mr Minshull's trial.

Richard Elmes (left), 51, delivered the punch that killed life-long friend Adrian Cooksey (right), 50, after a drinking session

The 51-year-old was arrested after police taped phone conversations to his wife.

He was found guilty of manslaughter today in the same trial and will be sentenced at a later date.

Judge Neil Ford told him a custodial sentence was inevitable but granted him bail to put his affairs in order.

Det Insp Ian Saunders, from Wiltshire Police, speaking after the verdict, said: 'For three years Richard Elmes continually and deliberately lied about his involvement to police.

'He lied under oath at the trial of a young man he knew was not responsible for Adrian's death.

'Adrian was assaulted in the early hours of March 8, 2009 in Spa Road, Melksham and later died from his injuries.

'The two men had been socialising together and were on their way home.

'There was an argument between them which resulted in Elmes delivering the blow which caused Adrian to fall and hit his head.

'Elmes then left the scene - he made no attempt to get Adrian any medical help.'

Elmes was found guilty of manslaughter at Bristol Crown Court today

Elmes said he was too drunk to remember what happened to Mr Cooksey and insisted the he had not been at the scene, in Melksham, Wiltshire, when the assault happened.

Suspicions were raised over Elmes' evidence and gaps in CCTV footage suggested he could have been there when Mr Cooksey, a builder, recieved the fatal blow.

A fresh investigation was launched and Elmes admitted 'telling porkies' to detectives and perjury in the previous case.

Bristol Crown Court was told how father-of-three Mr Cooksey had been discovered in a pool of blood by an off-duty PCSO at around 2.40am.

Adrian Cooksey ran a building firm and was a keen cricketer

Ian Lawrie QC, opening the case to the jury, said a pathologist's report showed how Mr Cooksey died from an overwhelming brain injury after a heavy blow to the face.

He described how Mr Cooksey and Elmes had been drinking with friend Andrew Fletcher before leaving to catch a taxi together.

Elmes initially told police he was 'too drunk' to remember what had happened after that.

Mr Lawrie said: 'It is the Crown's case that the moment police spoke to Elmes following the discovery of the body that Elmes wasn't being entirely honest and that he effectively told a number of lies.

'Those statements by Elmes had significant impact on the police investigation being carried out.'

Blurry CCTV footage shows where the incident happened

Crime scene: Police tape marks the road where the fatal punch was delivered in 2009

The phone recordings confirmed that Mr Elmes recalls much more than he let on to the police in earlier statements.

'They confirm he was in Spa Road and they confirm he had a verbal altercation Mr Cooksey,' said Lawrie.

A jury convicted Elmes of manslaughter following a two-week trial.

Speaking after the verdict Det Ch Supt Kier Pritchard, head of protective services at Wiltshire Police, said: 'We are pleased that the man responsible for Adrian Cooksey's death has been brought to justice.

'This investigation highlights the unwavering dedication of Wiltshire Police to uncover the truth.

A reward was being offered for more information on how Mr Cooksey died

'It is deeply regrettable that Thomas Minshull was put through the trauma of the investigation and the court process on the previous occasion where the Jury found he was not responsible and he was acquitted.

'We appreciate that this was a very distressing experience for him and his family who have continued to assist us and shown dignity throughout.

'We would like to extend our sincere apologies to Thomas and his family.'

Elmes was bailed to return for sentence on October 26th.
