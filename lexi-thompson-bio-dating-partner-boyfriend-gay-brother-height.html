Bio of Lexi Thompson

Lexi Thompson was born on February 10, 1995, in Coral Springs, Florida in the United States of America. Her birth name is Alexis Thompson, while her mother’s name is Amanda Thompson and her father’s name is Scott Thompson. She is an American nationality by birth and belongs to the white ethnic group.

It can be said that Lexi inherited her golfing talent from her father because he used to be a golfer who started playing at a very young age and later became a golf coach when he stopped competing. Her two brothers discussed below are also professional golfers.

For her education, Lexis was home-schooled during her high school years, and by September 2012 she had secured admission to Louisiana State University to earn a college degree. She must have graduated by now, but it is not yet known what degree she received from the educational institution.

Since Lexis was born into a family of golfers, she could not have considered it strange to play the sport, which is mainly dominated by men. She started playing at a very young age and qualified for the US Women’s Open in 2007 as the youngest player at the age of 12. She did not win the competition, but in the same year, she won the Aldila Junior Classic, becoming the second-youngest player in the American Junior Golf Association (AJGA) to win the title in addition to winning the Westfield Junior PGA Championship, as the youngest person ever at the time.

Her amateur golf laurels boast a total of 3 championship victories, including the 2003 and 2004 U.S. Kids Golf World Championship, 2008 U.S. Girls’ Junior, while her other successes as an amateur golfer include second round, U.S. Girls’ Junior in 2007, a quarter-finalist in U.S. Women’s Amateur of the same year, third round in 2009, U.S. Girls’ Junior and semi-finalist, U.S. Women’s Amateur of the same year.

Lexi Thompson began playing professional golf in 2010 and signed sponsorship agreements with Red Bull and Cobra-PUMA Golf. Her first three professional golf tournaments earned her $314,842. Her first professional win came on February 21, 2011, at a Fuzion Tour round-robin event at her home course in Coral Springs, Florida. In September she won her first LPGA tournament, the Navistar LPGA Classic at the age of 16, making her the youngest winner on the LPGA Tour at the time.

Lexi’s successes in her professional career boast 9 victories on the LPGA Tour, including Navistar LPGA Classic, Sime Darby LPGA Malaysia, Lorena Ochoa Invitational, Kraft Nabisco Championship, Meijer LPGA Classic, LPGA KEB Hana Bank Championship, Honda LPGA Thailand, Kingsmill Championship, and Indy Women in Tech Championship. Lexi has also won a LET victory (Dubai Ladies Masters) and an LPGA of Japan Tour victory (World Ladies Championship Salonpas Cup).

Over the years, she has continued to improve her position in the women’s golf world rankings at the end of each year. In 2009, Lexi was ranked 246th in the world rankings, but in 2017 she moved up to 4th in the rankings. As she is still young, very young, and still has plenty of room to improve her accuracy, it should come as no surprise when we see that in the not-too-distant future, she will be number one in the rankings.
