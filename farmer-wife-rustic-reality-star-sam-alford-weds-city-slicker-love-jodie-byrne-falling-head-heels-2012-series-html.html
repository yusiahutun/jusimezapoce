Farmer Wants A Wife's Sam Alford weds girlfriendJodie Byrne

 Posted on February 24, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  647 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

He signed up for reality TV show Farmer Wants A Wife in 2012 in the hope of finding a life partner.

And just over two years after he enchanted the nation with his onscreen romance with city girl Jodie Byrne, it looks as though the gamble has paid off for cattle farmer Sam Alford.

The Kununurra resident, 36, and his leading lady, sealed the deal on Saturday after they wed during an intimate ceremony on the banks of Shoalhaven River on the NSW South Coast.

'I do'! The Farmer Wants A Wife star Sam Alford wed his reality TV love Jodie Byrne on Saturday

The lovebirds exchanged vows in front of close family members and friends at a venue named Driftwood Shed.

The quaint outdoor space, located 140 kilometres from Jodie's hometown of Camden, south of Sydney, was likely chosen for its sentimental significance as well as its natural beauty after Sam popped the question while the couple were '[sitting] down on his giant piece of driftwood'.

Able to accommodate up to 100 guests, the daintily fitted out venue, which is so popular it's already fully booked out for weddings in 2015, was adorned with an array of floral arrangements, fairy lights and rustic decorations. 

A few years in the making: The couple were contestants on the show in 2012 and got engaged that Christmas 

Romantic: They said their vows in front of family and friends on the banks of Shoalhaven River

Guests converged on the property's extensive grass lawns to witness the outdoor ceremony.

For the reception meanwhile, the pair had the choice of an open-air seating area or spacious and gently lit marquee.

The dress code for the elegant affair was smart casual with Jodie recommending her gal pals wear wedges rather than stiletto heels on Facebook as they were 'on grass [the] majority of the time'. 

Her sister Aimee, who looked to be one of the bridesmaids, lead the fashion stakes in a floaty blush pink frock with an asymmetric neckline that went over one shoulder. 

Quaint: The love birds said 'I do' at rustic venue Driftwood Shed on the NSW South Coast

Romantic setting: The had the option of an outdoor reception or a more sheltered meal inside a marquee

Country views: The venue overlooks the very picturesque Shoalhaven River

Given that many of the attendees on Sam's side travelled from Western Australia to take part in the ceremony, overnight accommodation at the nearby Springs Resort was recommended by the couple.

And continuing the celebrations into the following day, guests again met at Driftwood Shed for a group breakfast in the morning. 

Jodie was one of three single city slickers who made the journey out to the Kimberly in WA in quest of love.

She eventually won Sam's heart over brunette stunner Ellen and fellow beauty Rebecca.

Bridesmaids in pink? Jodie's sister Aimee shared a photo of herself in a blush dress with an asymmetric hem after the wedding

Country convert: Jodie and cattle farmer Sam live together in WA

The tattooed musterer and cattleman was nominated to appear on the show by his best friend and proposed to Jodie at Christmas, just months after the series went to air in 2012.

Speaking about the moment he got down on one knee, Jodie told Yahoo 7 in February: 'We sat down on his giant piece of driftwood ... he looked a bit nervous'.

'I had no idea, I think until he started talking about me being his wife one day. 

'And I was like "are you proposing" and he was like "maybe I am" and then he pulled out the ring and it was real.'

Jodie has since moved onto Sam's ranch in WA. 

Real reality romance: The beauty won her beau's heart on the reality show

Chosen one: She was competing against fellow city slickers Ellen and Rebecca
