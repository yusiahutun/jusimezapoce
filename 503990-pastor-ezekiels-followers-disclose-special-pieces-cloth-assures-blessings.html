Pastor Ezekiel's Followers Disclose He Gives Them Special Pieces of Cloth that Assures Them Blessing

 Posted on March 30, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  610 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill
  • Followers of Pastor Ezekiel Odero's church argue the special articles of prayer he sells them have transformed their lives
  • The cleric prays for the scraps of cloth and branded bottled water before selling them to the faithful at KSh 100 per piece
  • Ezekiel has become a subject of national discourse due to his supposed links with Pastor Paul Mackenzie in whose church hundreds of followers have died mysteriously

Mavueni, Kilifi county - It now emerges Christian believers flock to New Life Prayer Centre and Church to get special articles of prayer which they believe bear blessings.

Pastor Ezekiel Odero is said to issue white pieces of cloth to Christians who troop his church for prayers.

The faithful of the church claims that once armed with the said scraps of cloth, their problems disappear immediately after they pray as per the instructions of their spiritual leader.

Special handkerchief and water

To accompany the cloth are bottles of holy water which Ezekiel prays for before releasing them to his flock at a fee.

In a past interview, Ezekiel revealed that the articles had healed many who bought them; a bottle and a piece of cloth are released at KSh 100 each.

"When I pray, I believe it has power and when they use i,t they are going to be healed. I believe this is more significant than going to the burial of my congregant.“These things (holy water and prayer cloth) we bulk buy from the shops, and when we do so, we spend money. So that it does not get to a point where the offertory we receive is not even enough to pay for electricity bills, we charge these items. We sell both at Sh100 everywhere,” he said.

Odero is presently in police custody as investigators carry out a probe into his conduct and likely links with Pastor Paul Mackenzie whose church has hit the headlines for the wrong reasons.

It is in Mackenzie's Good News International church that hundreds of congregants have died due to starvation packaged as fasting.

The duo's tribulations have continued; the latest to crack the whip would be the Communications Authority of Kenya which announced revoking the broadcasting rights of two television stations linked with them.

The agency's director-general Ezra Chiloba said World Evangelism and Times TV stations flouted the broadcasting standards and the conditions borne in their respective licenses.

New Life and Times TV stations suspended

The verdict to suspend the stations was realised following an extensive review of the kind of content aired by them between January and April this year.

It was established that the said stations were feeding their audience with inappropriate content during the watershed period.

"The Communications Authority of Kenya (CA) has suspended with immediate effect any further broadcasting by the World Evangelism TV (New Life Communications Ltd) and Times TV (Good News Media Kenya Ltd). Following an extensive review of content aired by the two stations between January-April 2023, the Authority established that the TV stations violated broadcasting standards' requirements and license conditions. World Evangelism TV was found to have aired inappropriate content on exorcism during the watershed period as well as 12 other violations. Similarly, Times TV aired inappropriate content on exorcism and flouted 17 other broadcasting license conditions," said the agency in an official communique.
