What is a total solar eclipse?

When the moon passes between the Earth and the sun, it casts a shadow over the sun—either partially or fully blocking its light. This happens twice a year. But solar eclipses can look completely different, depending on where you’re standing and where the sun, moon, and Earth are orbiting.

There are four kinds of solar eclipses:

  • Partial solar eclipse: The moon partially covers the sun, making it look like a crescent. This is the most common type of solar eclipse—we’ve had 77 since 2000. 
  • Total solar eclipse: The moon fully covers the sun, completely blocking its light. There have been 68 total solar eclipses since 2000. But until now, only one has been visible from the U.S.
  • Annular solar eclipse: The moon covers the sun—but because it’s so far away from Earth, it looks smaller than the sun. This creates a black circle (the moon) with a “ring of fire” (the sun) behind it. There have been 73 annular solar eclipses since 2000, but none have been visible in the U.S.
  • Hybrid solar eclipses: The Earth’s curved surface causes an eclipse to look like a total solar eclipse and an annular solar eclipse, depending on where the moon is in its orbit. Unsurprisingly, this is the rarest form of eclipse—with only seven hybrid eclipses since 2000 and none visible in the U.S. since 1930.

Monday’s solar eclipse is a total solar eclipse—one of the rarest kinds of eclipses you can see. As the moon completely covers the sun, the sky will darken, and the eclipse will look like a big black circle with luminescent rays around it.

When is the 2024 solar eclipse?

The next solar eclipse will take place on Monday, April 8 at 2:07 p.m. ET. Depending on where you are, the total solar eclipse could last anywhere from a few seconds to a few minutes. And for hours before and after the total solar eclipse, you should be able to see a partial solar eclipse—if you’re in the right spot and the sky is clear.

Where can you see the 2024 solar eclipse?

The eclipse will be visible across North America, but some places will get a better view than others. If you’re in the path of totality—where the total solar eclipse will be fully visible—you’ll see the moon completely cover the sun if you look at the right time. The path of totality includes U.S. cities like Dallas, Cleveland, and Buffalo, arcing up from Texas through Maine.

If you’re not in the path of totality, don’t fret. The total solar eclipse will look a little different for you, but you’ll still be able to see it. When the moon covers the sun, the sky will darken—but a sliver of the sun may be visible. This NASA eclipse map shows what the solar eclipse will look like at different times of day in different places, so you can plan exactly when to head outside and have a look.
