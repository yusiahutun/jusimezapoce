Photos: Tanzania Unveils Magnificent State House, 200 Times Bigger Than Old One

 Posted on January 29, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  494 words  |  Martina Birk

On Saturday, May 20, President Samia Suluhu Hassan of Tanzania unveiled the newly constructed State House in Chamwino, Dodoma, marking a significant milestone after a construction period of 30 months.

The Citizen Tanzania reports that construction of the building commenced in 2020, when late President John Magufuli laid the foundation stone for the commencement of the project.

Project envisioned by Nyerere in 1970s

The project marks the successful achievement of a vision by Tanzania's founding father Mwalimu Julius Nyerere, who chose the Chamwino spot in the 1970s.

During the reign of Magufuli, the man nicknamed bulldozer underpinned the need for government offices to relocate to Dodoma.

Official records show that the imposing structure sits on a parcel of land 200 times bigger than the State House in Dar es Salaam.

Conspicuous on the front porch are giant photos of the country's presidents dating back to Nyerere all the way to Suluhu.

Construction done by locals

The State House in Dar es Salaam was built by colonialists, however, the Chamwino State House boasts expertise and workforce from locals.

Details indicate that the government, through the National Service Wing of Tanzania People's Defence Forces (TPDF), built the structure.

Move to grow Dodoma as capital city

Apart from decongesting Dar es Salaam by moving government services, it will also spur the economic development of Dodoma as Tanzania's capital.

“The inauguration we are witnessing today is an official sign and confirmation of the completion of the government’s ambition to make Dodoma the capital of the United Republic of Tanzania for those who had doubts about the fulfilment of this activity,” Samia said during the event, as reported by Daily News.

She added that government officials will only go to Dar es Salaam to receive international guests for now, but the long-term idea is to let Dar become a business city.

State House launched by Samia

The building opened its doors for use in a pompous ceremony on Saturday, May 20, after 30 months of construction.

Proceedings started at dawn when various guests started to arrive at the grounds of the new seat of power from both mainland Tanzania and Zanzibar.

Cost of building not divulged

The government has, however, not shared details on how much was spent on the new building, which is a replica of the one in Dar es Salaam.

Among those who attended the launch are retired presidents Ali Hassan Mwinyi and Jakaya Mrisho Kikwete.

Samia, who was welcomed to the launch by Vice President, Dr. Philip Mpango, planted a tree in front of the Chamwino State House building.
