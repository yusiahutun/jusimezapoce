Who was Eli Stutzman?

Eli was born on September 30, 1950. He grew up in an Amish community in Apple Creek, Ohio. Not much is known about his formative years, though. People who knew Eli described him as a quiet and withdrawn man.

Stutzman was married to Ida, but his wife died in a ban fire. She was pregnant at the time of her death. They also had a 10-month-old son, Danny. According to Eli, his wife went to the barn to save milking equipment and collapsed. The death was ruled as an accident at the time.

After his wife’s passing, Eli fell into depression. Later, he shaved his beard, going against Amish tradition and started using electricity at his residence. Shortly after, in 1982, Eli moved to Colorado with his son.

How did Eli die?

Eli was found dead in his apartment in Ft. Worth, Texas, on a Wednesday afternoon in 2007. According to The Tarrant County (Texas) Medical Examiner's Officer, Eli killed himself. The cause of death was a sharp force injury to the left arm.

Glen Pritchett and Danny Stutzman

On May 12, 1985, the body of Glen Pritchett was discovered lying by the side of a Texas road. Glen had been shot numerous times. Later, police officers found out that he was shot by his roommate, Eli Stutzman, but he had fled the state by the time of the discovery.

Things even got worse on Christmas eve later that year; people found a young boy's body by the side of the road in Nebraska. Unfortunately, the authorities could not identify the boy and state how he died. The case went cold for two years. Considering the boy's unidentified state, he was dubbed "Little Boy Blue."

Readers Digest published a story about the case in 1987, which got the nation's attention. And a woman from Wyoming, who thought she knew the boy came forward. She claimed that she took care of him a few months before his father picked him up, days before he was found dead.

David Tyler and Dennis Slaetter

While police were looking into Stutzman, they became aware of two other unsolved murders in Durango, Colorado. The same year Eli's son and Glen died, David Tyler was shot outside his autobody shop. Dennis Slaetter was also murdered at the liquor store where he worked. All these people were close associates of Eli Stutzman.

According to police records, David resided in Durango for three years and was one of the three owners of an automotive shop. He worked as a manager and mechanic. Tyler was openly gay and told friends he received death threats from someone who hated homosexuals. The threats scared him, and he had to carry a handgun.

Dennis, on the other hand, was only two weeks away from earning a degree in industrial psychology from Fort Lewis College when he was shot dead.

The police concluded that Dennis and Tyler knew each other, were drug users and were gay. Eli, the prime suspect in the case, was in Durango at the time of the murders and was also gay. Someone fitting his description was seen near Dennis' scene of death. However, Eli Stutzman was never charged with the murders because of a lack of evidence.
