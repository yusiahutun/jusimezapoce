Media career

The television personality currently works at Fox News, where he earns an impressive salary. He has gradually climbed the ranks in his career that started over 20 years ago. His career timeline is explored below.

  • He started his career at a national conservative journal called Policy Review before becoming a writer for the Arkansas Democrat-Gazette newspaper.
  • In 1995, he joined The Weekly Standard news magazine. Later, he became a columnist for New York magazine and Reader's Digest.
  • Between 2000 and 2005, he worked at CNN. He co-hosted The Spin Room and Crossfire.
  • Between 2004 and 2005, he hosted Tucker Carlson: Unfiltered on PBS.
  • From 2005 to 2008, he worked for MSNBC. Initially, he was a show host but later became a senior campaign correspondent.
  • In May 2009, he joined Fox News Channel, where he works to date. He is known for hosting the Tucker Carlson Tonight show.

The Daily Caller

In 2010, the media personality and Neil Patel co-founded a website called The Daily Caller. It is right-wing news and opinion site that is considered the conservative version of The Huffington Post.

Foster Freiss, a conservative activist, funded the website. The site did well and earned the founders money. In June 2020, he sold a third of his stake in the site to Patel. The amount of money he made from this sale remains undisclosed.

Tucker Carlson's inheritance

The journalist's parents separated when he was six years old, and his father got custody of his two boys. Later, his father married Patricia Caroline Swanson.

Patricia, the daughter of Gilbert Carl Swanson, was the heiress to Swanson Enterprises, commonly known as Swanson Foods. However, by the time Patricia married the journalist's father, the family had already sold Swansons to the Campbell Soup Company. This means that Swanson Foods does not belong to the journalist or his wife.

In 2011, his biological mother, Lisa, passed away. She was the beneficiary of an oil and gas partnership in rural areas of central California, so she left behind some wealth. For years now, he and his brother Buckley have been in a lawsuit battle related to their late mother's estate.
