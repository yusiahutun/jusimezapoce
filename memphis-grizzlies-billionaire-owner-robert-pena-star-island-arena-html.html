Memphis Grizzlies' billionaire owner Robert Pera angers neighbors by turning home on super-wealthy M

 Posted on October 7, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1135 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

A billionaire NBA owner has reached a tipping point with his super-wealthy Miami Beach neighbors after building a colossal mega mansion boasting a basketball ‘arena’ and a jumbotron.

The under-construction home belonging to Memphis Grizzlies chairman Robert Pera, 45, has a professional-grade court, bleachers, locker rooms and a video screen so large that locals can see it from half a mile away.  

Exclusive DailyMail.com images show how the 28,000 square-foot, glass-fronted structure on exclusive Star Island dwarfs surrounding homes belonging to the likes of J.Lo, Diddy and Rick Ross.


Pera, worth an estimated $7.1 billion, had to tweak his design and hire a lobbyist to appease city planners after his nearest neighbor accused him of building a ‘professional basketball facility’ with more locker rooms than bedrooms.

The massive basketball arena's design comprises a wedge-shaped main house attached to a huge hangar

Residents living across the bay in Miami Beach say the dazzling jumbotron has been switched on continuously, day and night, for the past week

Pera (left at a Grizzlies playoff game) paid $377 million for the team in 2012, becoming the youngest controlling owner in the NBA

The latest addition to the palatial pad’s vast open-plan living area is a two-story high, 40-foot wide jumbotron that wouldn’t look out of place among the giant screens at the Grizzlies’ FedExForum arena in downtown Memphis.

Residents living across the bay in Miami Beach say the dazzling display has been switched on continuously, day and night, for the past week or so, beaming out video clips of forests, beaches and cityscapes and flooding the night skies with light.

‘Even by Miami standards, this place is on another level. It’s a goliath,’ said accountant Sal Galbo, 46. 

'It looks more like a sports facility or a hotel than a house.

'The way it’s lit up at night you could probably make it out from outer space. The electricity bill alone must be astronomical.'

California native Pera started his own computer company in high school and played on the basketball team until a rare heart condition kept him home for a year.

He joined Apple but left in 2005 to start wireless equipment maker Ubiquiti Networks, earning a spot on the Forbes list of 10 youngest billionaires in his mid-30s.

The lifelong basketball fan paid $377 million for the Grizzlies in 2012, becoming the youngest controlling owner in the NBA and steering the team to a club-record 56 wins in his first year.

Pera went on another spending spree in 2019, snapping up property in New York, Seattle and a $25 million spread in exclusive Star Island, a tiny private neighborhood of 33 homes nestling between Miami and Miami Beach.

He bulldozed the existing seven-bed, 10,211-square foot mansion and, according to public records, hired a lobbyist to help persuade planning board officials to greenlight a structure roughly three times larger.

The tech mogul sought special permission to build up to 31 feet (three feet higher than local limits) to allow him to construct a ‘professional grade indoor basketball court’ his attorney, Tracy Slavens, told a June 2021 Miami Beach board meeting.

‘This is a hobby of the owner. He likes to play at all hours,’ she said, according to an account of the meeting published by property magazine, The Real Deal.

'The way it’s lit up at night you could probably make it out from outer space,' one neighbor told DailyMail.com. 'The electricity bill alone must be astronomical'

From half a mile away across the Intracoastal Waterway the dazzling jumbotron can clearly be seen in front of the Miami skyline

Papers presented to Miami Beach City showed that Pera hired a lobbying firm to help him get the plans for the building approved

The radical design comprising a wedge-shaped main house attached to a huge hangar was opposed by Vlad Doronin, a billionaire property developer who bought the neighboring home from Shaquille O’Neal for $16 million in 2009.

Doronin’s attorney, Neisen Kasdin, told the board that Pera’s proposed digs resembled a sports complex rather than a home and that the extra height and position of the balconies would invade his client’s privacy.

Pera, 45, made his fortune by founding Ubiquiti Networks, earning a spot on the Forbes list of 10 youngest billionaires while in his mid-30s

‘This looks like, smells like, and quacks like a professional basketball facility,’ Kasdin said, claiming the structure had locker rooms for ‘an entire basketball team and its entourage’ yet only three bedrooms.

‘We have made multiple attempts through the owner’s attorney … to contact and reach the owner to discuss what he’s seeking [to build] and see if there’s a way to mitigate or modify it,’ added Kasdin, a former Miami Beach mayor.

Slavens insisted there were seven bedrooms but withdrew requests for the three-foot variance on height and other exemptions covering the roof deck and perimeter wall, The Real Deal reported. ‘We are doing this to avoid a fight with the neighbor,’ she said.

The board approved the plans 5-2, allowing Pera to install a new electrical substation and an elevator bulkhead reaching 13ft above the roofline that would allow him to fit an elevator for his elderly father.

When DailyMail.com surveyed the property this week it was teeming with construction workers, one of whom confirmed the home does indeed have a basketball court with rows of seats akin to bleachers.

There’s a tennis court, pool, boat dock and roof deck offering stunning views of the Intracoastal Waterway plus a brightly lit atrium that resembles a museum or gallery with rows of giant solar panels installed on its roof.

Pera welcomed Justin Timberlake to a game at the FedExForum in Memphis in 2014. Timberlake holds a minority stake in the team

The Grizzlies beat the Dallas Mavericks 120-103 in their most recent game on Tuesday with guard Desmond Bane (22) leading the scoring with 39 points

Locals in Miami jokingly compare Pera's arena on Star Island to the Grizzlies' home, The FedExForum in Memphis

The giant screen was switched on despite nobody appearing to live at the gaudy 1.9-acre property, which looks far from finished.

‘He’s got the world’s biggest TV switched on 24/7 in his living room,’ said coder Sam Hastings, 39, who lives directly across the bay.

‘Like the majority of Miami Beach residents, the man comes across as both tasteful and environmentally conscious,' he added sarcastically.

Star Island ranked as the most expensive residential neighborhood in the US in sales data released by Zillow last March, with the average house fetching an eye-watering $40.2 million in 2022.

According to The Wall Street Journal, Pera’s other homes include a $21.55 million Manhattan penthouse and a $3 million apartment in the Four Seasons Residences overlooking Puget Sound in Seattle.

Lawyers for Pera and Doronin did not respond to requests for comment.
