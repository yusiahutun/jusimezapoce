Pictured: Child, 9, is fatally crushed by a metal gate she was helping close at her Arizona Elementa

 Posted on September 17, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  558 words  |  Martina Birk

A little girl was crushed and killed by a metal gate that she was helping close at her elementary school in Tuscon, Arizona, this week.

The nine-year-old, named Arlette, was remembered for her 'positive attitude and funny demeanor' as her family said that she died at favorite place in the world - her school.

Paramedics rushed to Centennial Elementary School on Friday afternoon to revive the child - but she was pronounced dead at a hospital. 

Police said that Arlette was helping close a large metal gate when it disconnected and fell on top of her.  

The Pima County Sheriff's Department said the fatal ordeal happened at 2.22pm.

Flowing Wells Unified School District said in a letter to parents: 'This is a tragic event, and we are all grieving.'

The district said counselors will support students and staff at the school on Monday, grieving the loss of little Arlette. 

Her family, who remembered her as a wonderful, cheerful girl, are raising money for funeral costs after the 'sudden loss' that they were not prepared for.  

The GoFundMe read: 'It is with great sadness, our beloved Arlette has recently passed away due to a horrific accident at her favorite place in the world, school. 

'Arlette was a wonderful daughter, sister, granddaughter, and friend to many. She always touched the lives of those around her, had the biggest smile on her face, and was always cheerful.

'Arlette was just 9 years old and had a very bright future ahead of her. She was a great student who was loved by her classmates and teachers.

'She was very active in school and had just won second place in cross country. She always made her family smile with her positive attitude and funny demeanor. 

'She will be greatly missed by all the people she has touched and most importantly her family.

'We are all devastated by Arlette's sudden loss and were not prepared for the high cost of a funeral service. We want to give Arlette the memorial she deserves to honor her memory and say our final goodbyes.

'Anything would help and would be greatly appreciated. Please share this around with your friends and family and help us spread this gofundme to help us honor our beloved Arlette with the proper send-off. Thank you in advance, god bless.'

Schreen Davis, who is a teacher in the district, wrote on social media: 'Please share and help this wonderful family. Arlette's passing has shattered our Centennial Elementary School community. Our hearts break for them.'

Tyler Kiecolt, another teacher, wrote: 'Such a sweet girl. Absolutely heartbreaking.' 

Arlette's family members have been contacted for comment.
