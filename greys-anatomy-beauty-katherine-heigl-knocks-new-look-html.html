Grey's Anatomy beauty Katherine Heigl knocks up a new brunette look

Updated: 07:38 EST, 21 July 2009

Knocked Up star Katherine Heigl has ditched her Fifties-style golden curls in favour of a drastic new brunette 'do.

The 30-year-old actress has rebelled against the producers of medical TV drama Grey's Anatomy by dyeing her hair brown after years of maintaining her blonde colour.

As her character Dr Izzie Stevens is currently bald after undergoing brain surgery , Katherine has finally fulfilled her dream of going brunette after being forbidden to do so by the producers of the show.

Going to the dark side: Katherine Heigl shows off her new brunette hairdo in New York yesterday (left) after ditching her previous blonde style

Following the footsteps of recent blondes-turned-brunettes Scarlett Johansson and Kate Hudson, the stunning actress showed off her auburn new crop as she appeared on the Late Show with David Letterman last night.

The actress looked sleek in a blue satin dress with black heels as she arrived at the Ed Sullivan Theatre in New York to promote her new film The Ugly Truth.

Katherine admitted she is thrilled to be brunette after originally begging producers to make her character a brunette when Grey's Anatomy first started four years ago.

She said: 'This is for fun, because on the show I don't have hair. Now I can do whatever I want.'

Rhapsody in blue: Katherine complimeted her new look with a blue satin dress as she arrived at the Ed Sullivan Theatre in New York

In an interview with Cosmopolitan magazine two years ago, she said: 'My plan was to be a brown-haired Izzie, but they said no.

'I'd love to be a brunette. Having brown hair makes me feel more exotic.'

Katherine has recently returned to the set of Grey's Anatomy to begin filming the sixth season after a dramatic cliff-hanger last series left fans wondering if her character would survive surgery to remove a tumour from her brain.

During her time out from the show, Katherine has been filming the thriller Five Killers with Ashton Kutcher as well as promoting romantic comedy The Ugly Truth with Scots co-star Gerard Butler.

Blonde ambition: A flaxen-haired Katherine filming Five Killers in May

Promoting the film, Katherine admitted she was pleased it had received a R rating (under 18s must be accompanied by a parent of guardian), even though it means less people can see the movie.

She told InStyle magazine: 'I know this is going to sound bad but I'm really glad it's an R, I didn't want to do another PG-13 movie.

'You're probably going to drop the F-bomb once in a while. You're probably going to say some things that are kind of harsh. And you're definitely going to talk about sex.

'It doesn't have to be so Snow White and Prince Charming. That's my problem with a lot of romantic comedies: Everyone spends so much time trying to make sure it's the fairytale because that's what the rating calls for.'

The Ugly Truth stars Katherine as a romantically challenged morning show producer who becomes embroiled in a series of outrageous tests by her chauvinistic correspondent (Gerard) to prove his theories on relationships and help her find love.
