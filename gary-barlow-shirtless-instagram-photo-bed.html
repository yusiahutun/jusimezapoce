    Gary Barlow might have only recently joined Instagram, but he's already causing a stir on the social media site. On Monday morning, the star shared a shirtless photograph of himself in bed, following a successful night at the WhatsOnStage Awards on Sunday. The photo, which has unsurprisingly proved popular with fans, shows 46-year-old Gary lying with his eyes closed while clutching the trophy in his hand. He wrote: "Look what I woke up with! A great night at the What's On Stage awards. Delighted to win an award. Cast and crew all deserve it. What a warm up for tonight's gala night… #thegirlsmusical."

At the ceremony, they won the prize for Best Regional Production and received a rapturous reception from the audience as they made their way onto the stage. is based on the true story of the Yorkshire Calendar Girls, and the 2003 British comedy film , which Tim also wrote the screenplay for.

    It's been a very busy year for Gary so far. As well as working on the West End production, he has been starring as a judge on new TV singing competition,. Gary appears on the judging panel alongside Dannii Minogue and Martin Kemp in the search for five men to star in a new musical based on the songs of Take That. He recently spoke to HELLO! about the project, and said that his former bandmates – Mark Owen, Robbie Williams, Jason Orange and Howard Donald - were all on board.

    "We're all so excited," he said. "We're all producers of the show. This is our baby, really. It's a celebration of our career. Hopefully this music will live on way beyond our capability to perform. When we're in wheelchairs being pushed around an old folks' home, this musical will still be touring." The final will air this week, with guest judge Robbie Williams joining the panel.
