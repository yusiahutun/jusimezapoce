Insanity workout: meaning, examples and effects

 Posted on February 17, 2024  |  11 minutes  |  2225 words  |  Martina Birk

For most novices, engaging in a workout called “insanity” does not sound or look enjoyable. It sounds insane. In addition, the ‘street’ dictionary defines insanity as doing the same thing several times expecting different results. So, why would anybody think of doing an insanity workout? Well, those who have done it ascertain that it is the best and most challenging workout program out there. Since its launch in 2009 by Shaun T, the insanity workout has been popular all over the world.

But what is the insanity workout? What does it entail, exactly? Does it have any effects on the body?

What is insanity workout?

Insanity workout is a 60-day fitness program that does not require equipment or going to the gym. It entails subjecting your body to immense stress for 30 minutes, then resting for a few seconds and then continuing with the exercises. Essentially, you will be doing high-intensity resistance and aerobic exercises for six days a week.

The program has two phases. You must complete them within 60 days or based on the insanity workout schedule provided. The first phase has five workouts. These include sequences that focus on explosive movements (lower-body plyometrics), abs, cardio, upper body strength, and recovery workout. They consist of:

  • The Fit Test
  • Plyometric Cardio Circuit
  • Cardio Power and Resistance
  • Cardio Recovery
  • Pure Cardio

The second phase has four different exercises. They include:

  • Max Interval Circuit
  • Max Interval Plyo
  • Max Cardio Conditioning
  • Max Recovery

Each workout starts with a warm-up. You will perform different movements like jumping jacks, jogging, and jump rope. After the warm-up, you will do some stretching. While doing a warm-up may seem elementary, in the insanity workout, it is not. If you are not in any physical form, it will leave you gasping for air.

There are no complex movements in the insanity workout. However, the pace and number of workouts make each interval hard to complete. The moves you will be making are variations of plyometrics, pushups, jumping jacks, squats, sprints, lunges, and kickboxing moves.

How long is the Insanity workout?

The insanity workout takes 60 days to complete. In these sixty days, you will do two phases of the workout as described above. Because you will be training for six days in a week, you will break down your exercises in phase one into several minutes as follows:

  • The Fit Test – 26 minutes
  • Plyometric Cardio Circuit – 42 minutes
  • Cardio Power and Resistance – 40 minutes
  • Cardio Recovery – 33 minutes
  • Pure Cardio – 39 minutes

For phase two, the following is the length of each exercise you will be doing.

  • Max Interval Circuit – 60 minutes
  • Max Interval Plyo – 56 minutes
  • Max Cardio Conditioning – 48 minutes
  • Max Recovery – 47 minutes

Effects of the insanity workout

The effects of the program depend on the insanity workout results you want. This is a fitness program that consists of high-intensity workouts. That means you will burn fat, increase your metabolism, and increase your heart rate.

If your goal is to increase your muscle mass, the workout will not work for you. The insanity workout helps to develop cardiovascular endurance, improve muscle endurance, and increase power. That means it does not emphasize muscle strength. To build your muscle mass, you will need to increase your protein intake and lift heavy weights. You do not need any equipment when doing the insanity workout.

Insanity workout routine

Insanity workout is great if you want to start a fitness routine. But because you will be doing it at home, and it is hard to complete, it can be difficult to measure how effective the program is. If your goal is to lose weight, these are the effects you are likely to get in one month of training.

Week one

You are less likely to lose weight in your first week of insanity workout. Several things can contribute to this.

  • Poor diet
  • Busy monthly cycle
  • Your body’s adjustment to the program

To start losing weight or move the scale higher, you need a better eating routine in week two and commit to the workout. As for week one results, you will notice an increase in body definition.

Week two

If you are committed to the workout and have a proper diet, you will notice changes by week two. You should drop at least three pounds at this point. If you are a woman, you should see vertical lines on your abdominal external oblique muscle. Also, your upper abs should start to flatten and rest close to the body.

Week three

Week four

You should see some significant changes to your body in week four. By now, you should have lost six to eight pounds of weight by this time.

To determine whether insanity workout helps you to lose weight, first understand how the body loses fat. Your body loses fat when you burn fat. At the same time, you need to take in less energy to allow the body to burn more fat. The body draws off triglycerides from fat stores and breaks them down until they can be used as fuel.

The process should frequently happen to ensure the fat cells shrink and no longer have many triglycerides. At this point, your waistline should get smaller. Remember that the vital part of the process is burning more energy and doing lots of cardiovascular exercises. So, how does insanity workout contribute to all this?

The insanity workout employs circuit training, plyometrics, and interval training to increase heart rate and maintain it for the duration of the exercises. Since you will be losing weight, the strength exercises incorporated in the workout helps your body to retain a lean muscle mass.

What about diet?

The insanity workout is not all about high-intensity exercises. It also includes a diet plan. A diet plan helps to fuel your fitness exercises and lose fat. Its focus is to ensure you eat enough carbs that will boost your endurance. Also, you will need lean proteins to help repair your muscles.

Doing an intense exercise increases your appetite. But you need a proper diet because an unadvised snack can make your entire fitness plan look useless. To maximize the amount of fat you lose from doing the workouts, ensure to eat a low-fat diet.

A crucial point to note is that the insanity workout is not for everybody. Your physical fitness must be good in shape or condition. The workout keeps the heart rate within 80-90% of the body’s maximum heart rate throughout the exercises. As such, you must consult with your doctor first before you start the program. You must first complete a fit test before you start the program. If you cannot finish it, the intensity of the workout will be too much for you.

How many calories do you burn in an insanity workout?

The number of calories the body burns while doing the insanity workout will depend on many factors. These include:

Current weight

Your current weight will affect the number of calories you will burn doing the insanity workout. If you weigh 150 pounds, for example, you will lose about 100 calories if you do the exercises for 15-20 minutes.

If you weigh more, your body will need more energy to function. Heavier people will burn more calories doing this exercise than lighter people. The same case applies if you are overweight or you have a higher muscle mass.

Age

When a person ages, the number of muscles in their body tends to decrease. That means they have more fat that accounts for their total body weight. As such, the number of calories they can burn while doing the insanity workout is low because of less muscle tissue.

When you start losing your muscle tissue, your metabolism will slow down. To avoid this, you need to maintain your muscle tissue. You can achieve this by doing strength-training workouts at least three days a week.

Maximum heart rate

The topic on maximum heart rate and calorie burn when doing the insanity workout is complex. If your heart rate is high, the heart will not have enough time to keep up with the exercises. For example, it is difficult to go beyond 80% of your MHR (maximal heart rate) and maintain it for more than one minute. It is only possible if you are an athlete who has conditioned the body for it.

Counting how many calories the body can burn per one unit of time will vary drastically. Assume that you are exercising at 80 per cent of your MHR, but you can only do it for sixty seconds. That means if you take your MHR over 300 seconds, it will vary with that of an athlete who exercises for the full 300 seconds. But if you maintain your exercises for 300 seconds, you will burn more calories if your MHR is between 60 and 70%.

Gender – male or female

If a man and a woman do the insanity workout at the same time, the man will lose more calories than the woman. Men have lean muscle tissues that help them to burn more calories than fat. On average, a woman has between 6 and 11% more body fat than a man.

The extra fat helps them during pregnancy. Even if a woman takes in fewer calories, her average body fat will still be more than that of a man. Because men have larger body sizes, they will burn more calories than women.

Muscle mass

A pound of muscle and pound of muscle have the same weight. But their energy requirements are different. If you have more muscles, you will burn more calories than fat when doing the insanity workout.

If you are heavily muscular, it means you have a higher basal metabolic rate. That also means your body burns more calories even when at rest. One pound of muscle can help you burn around ten calories.

Genes

Genes can affect your weight in many ways. If your body is genetically predisposed to adding weight, you will have a difficult time trying to control your hunger. No matter how much time you put into doing the insanity workout, you will find yourself eating unhealthy foods. For example, you will eat a cookie which tends to have more calories. You will be burning more calories but retaking them through the foods you eat.

Rest period

Part of the insanity workout program is to allow the body to rest. Your rest period is essential because it determines the number of calories you will burn. For example, you will burn around 140 calories per hour while eating and 75 calories while reading or watching TV.

Any insanity workout review you read online will inform you that this is one of the most effective fitness exercises on the market. Any fitness program you begin that your body is not used to will have positive effects on it. Since the body is not used to the physical stimulus of the exercises, it will be forced to change and adapt. If you are not careful, it will break down through injury.

When you start the insanity workout, you will see positive changes. But for this to happen, you also have a proper diet plan. That is why the workout also comes with a diet plan to ensure the body gets the proper nutrition and fuel to enable it to continue with the exercises.
