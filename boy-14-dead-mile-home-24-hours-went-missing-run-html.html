Boy is found dead a mile from his home less than 24 hours after he went missing

 Posted on July 2, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  414 words  |  Larita Shotwell

A 14-year-old boy from Long Island was found dead from an apparent suicide Wednesday morning, less than a day after he was reported missing. 

Nickolas Donnelly left his parents' home in Shoreham at around 10am to go for a run. He was not carrying his cellphone or identification card at the time. 

When Nickolas failed to return home, Suffolk County police launched a large-scale search for him involving volunteers, helicopters and K-9 units, which came to a tragic end just before 8am Wednesday.   

Missing teen: Nickolas Donnelly, 14 (pictured left and right), was found dead in Shoreham, New York, Wednesday morning, less than 24 hours after his disappearance 

Heartbreaking find: Donnelly's body was found in this wooded area near Royal Way, a mile from his family's home  

The teenager's body was discovered in a wooded area near Royal Way, about a mile away from his family's home on Chambord Court, according to police.

Investigators say the cause of death appears to be 'non-criminal,' but they would not elaborate. NBC New York reported, citing unnamed sources, that the boy appears to have taken his own life.  

Nickolas' uncle, Donald Donnelly Jr, issued a statement on behalf of the family saying that the boy's parents were awaiting the autopsy results from the medical examiner's office, and that they were grateful for the support of the community, reported ABC 7 News.

Mr Donnelly also said that Nickolas will be missed by his two brothers, Kyle and Luke, and many friends.

Nickolas was home from school Tuesday because of the Jewish holiday of Rosh Ha'Shanah, PIX11 reported.

He was a ninth-grader at Wading River High School, where his uncle said he was an excellent student and standout athlete in two sports - wresting and baseball.

Mr Donnelly also noted that his nephew was popular and was not bullied.

'He always greeted you with a smile on his face and went out of his way to say hello,' Mr Donnelly's statement to the press read.
