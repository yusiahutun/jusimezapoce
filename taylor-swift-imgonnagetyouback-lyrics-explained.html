Lyrics to &quot;imgonnagetyouback&quot; by Taylor Swift, Explained

 Posted on August 13, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  689 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Breakups are rarely ever clean, and Taylor Swift embraces the mess in an understated bonus track from her newly released 11th studio album, The Tortured Poets Department.

In “imgonnagetyouback,” Swift sings about winning over an old flame following their split—though she’s unsure whether or not such a reunion will lead to a rekindled romance or another falling-out. As she sings in the chorus, “Whether I’m gonna be your wife or / Gonna smash up your bike, I haven’t decided yet / But I’m gonna get you back / Whether I’m gonna curse you out or / Take you back to my house, I haven’t decidеd yet / But I’m gonna get you back.”

Fans have speculated that much of The Tortured Poets Department references her six-year relationship with actor Joe Alwyn, and her subsequent dalliance with the 1975 frontman Matty Healy. It’s possible that “imgonnagetyouback” refers to either or both of these exes.

One line in particular can easily be interpreted as an allusion to Alwyn: “Bygones will be bygone, eras fadin’ into gray.” Swift is known for using colors to describe her relationship to Alwyn in songs, and has previously mentioned gray on the Midnights bonus song “You’re Losing Me” and the title track of her 2020 album Evermore.
