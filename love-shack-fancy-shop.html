Everything you need to know about LoveShackFancy

 Posted on March 17, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  1048 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Each week, we look into an exciting and innovative label that is taking the fashion world by storm in our regular feature #TheBrand. This time, we’re turning the spotlight on bohemian, vintage-inspired label LoveShackFancy.

Launched by former fashion editor Rebecca Cohen in 2013, the US brand has become renowned for its ditsy retro prints, breezy white cotton sun dresses and cosy knitted cardigans. Now with childrenswear, swimwear and nightwear under its belt, and with bridal and interiors coming next, the label is morphing into much more than the dress label it once was, heading into the direction of a full-blown lifestyle brand. We caught up with Cohen to find out how she got here and what might be next.

Like many of the most successful fashion brands, Cohen launched her label after spotting a gap in the market. When planning her wedding, she found that there were no bridesmaid dresses that captured what she was looking for, so she decided to make her own.

"My mum and I decided to design these dreamy silk halter dresses that captured an ethereal but sexy sensibility," she explained. "Everyone loved them and asked to purchase so I started hand-dying dresses in my backyard with my mum – playing around with a soft muted palette and selling them at trunk shows throughout the Hamptons."

From here, the label took off quickly. Cohen created a capsule of eight hand-dyed silk dresses and separates – and then collaborated with the likes of Gwyneth Paltrow for Goop and Julia Roitfeld for Romy. The following year, Cohen launched at Net-a-Porter and Barney's with a collection of black and white Victorian-inspired pieces. Now, the brand has become an international success story, with two stores in the US (and more to come), while also selling online across the globe at some of the most well-known retailers.

Part of Cohen's success, she believes, is down to the label's instantly recognisable prints and unique vintage inspiration.

"A key for us is that we use custom hand-painted florals and draw inspiration from vintage. Garden and flowers have been a heavy influence for LoveShackFancy since the start and our signature prints have now made us stand out from every other brand as we always believe more is more is more."

Many of these prints and designs have come about through the discovery of vintage pieces from across the globe. This, plus the creative atmosphere in which she grew up, has led to Cohen's unique point of view with fashion, she explains.

"Our collections are inspired by my childhood, travel, art and literature. Drawing from my travels, I'm influenced by the stories and the history of the places we go, from Provence and the English countryside to Marrakech and Spain. Plus, I scour flea markets in the United States and Europe on our trips for vintage fabrics, colours, patterns, textiles, and decorative ribbons to incorporate into our designs.

"Growing up with my mother, the creative director of Seventeen magazine for almost 20 years, meant I was constantly surrounded by photography and editorials. Plus, it was the era of Laura Ashley and she would dress us in matching Victorian and Edwardian vintage pieces. Nothing much has changed since then – I still dress the same and I'm still surrounded by imagery. I suppose it's part of my DNA."

And, when looking at how the label has expanded, this is clearly the case. Cohen has branched out into new areas that reflect where she is in her life, which gives the label a sense of authenticity.

"The brand has found its rhythm and has become more grown up, as I have – hemlines have gotten longer as I have gotten older. Pieces continue to become more elevated, introducing new kinds of craftsmanship as we create our collections."

With the brand's marketing too, much of this is based on the founder's life and aesthetic: "Our social media is derived from my life. I handle every image, and it is all a reflection of how I feel and what I want to come across in the collections."

Over the years, Cohen has introduced various new elements to the label, including collections for girls, which was partly inspired by her new life as a mother and discovering new avenues with work as she raised her children.

"I take what I want and apply it to what I think our customer wants. And as my life changes, as I evolve and grow, our brand evolves with it. Now I have two little girls, aged three and six, so I see what their needs are, as well as what mine are as a woman and a mom. I’m inspired by the young 20-year-olds around me as well, and I see how they live, and what they want to wear. What transports them into this sensibility is what I’m interested in."

LoveShackFancy's growth feels like a true reflection and reaction to what is happening in Cohen's life, as well as a slow and steady process. Next up, she will be moving into bridal and home, which she describes as "organic extensions of the brand". This is the next step in what will ultimately offer a full LoveShackFancy lifestyle to their customer.

Courtesy

"The future for us is retail, where we can connect with our customers by giving them the full lifestyle experience, which is inspired by each location. As we expand, whether its in matching your iPhone case to your bags to your bows or with our home collection, I think of it like a new modern take on Laura Ashley."

Perhaps part of the appeal of LoveShackFancy to bring it into this all-round experience is because it isn't trend led, but has its own specific feel, which won't date. In an age where waste and sustainability are at the forefront of many consumer's minds, the brand has a unique point of view, which is not hooked on buying an 'It' piece for just one season or following a catwalk moment, instead it is inspired by vintage pieces that transcend this.

"I like to think that LoveShackFancy is a brand that’s an investment, it can be passed on for generations – to your daughters, friends, I want every dress to tell a story."
