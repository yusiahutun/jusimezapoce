Chester FC: A Brief History

Chester FC, founded in 2010, is a semi-professional football club based in Chester, England. The club was formed by Chester City fans after the original Chester City FC was dissolved in the same year. Since its formation, Chester FC has been competing in the National League North, the sixth tier of the English football league system. The club’s aim is to restore football to the city of Chester and achieve success both on and off the pitch.

The Deva Stadium: Chester FC’s Home Ground

Chester FC plays its home matches at the Deva Stadium, which has a capacity of approximately 5,500 spectators. The stadium, located in the Bumpers Lane area of Chester, has been the club’s home since its formation. Named after the nearby River Dee, the Deva Stadium provides a great atmosphere for both home and away supporters.

A Fan-Driven Club

Chester FC prides itself on being a fan-driven club, with supporters actively involved in the running of the club. The “City Fans United” ownership model ensures that fans have a say in decision-making processes. Regular fan engagement events and initiatives are held to strengthen the bond between the club and its supporters.

Success on the Pitch

Chester FC has experienced success on the pitch since its formation. In the 2012-2013 season, the club won the Northern Premier League Premier Division title, securing promotion to the National League North. The team has also enjoyed several notable cup runs, including reaching the second round of the FA Cup.

The Blues and Whites

Chester FC is known for playing in their traditional blue and white striped home kit. The blue and white colors represent the club’s heritage and are synonymous with the club’s identity. The team wears all blue for away matches.

Rivalries

One of Chester FC’s biggest rivalries is with Wrexham AFC, another football club from North Wales. Matches between the two teams, known as the “North Wales Derby,” are fiercely contested and draw large crowds. These encounters are always highly anticipated by fans of both clubs.

Record Attendance

The highest recorded attendance at a Chester FC home match is approximately 5,700 spectators. This record was set during a highly anticipated league match against Tranmere Rovers in the 2016-2017 season. The passionate support from the crowd created an electrifying atmosphere at the Deva Stadium.

Affiliation with Liverpool FC

Chester FC has a close affiliation with Liverpool FC. This partnership allows talented young players from Liverpool’s academy to join Chester FC on loan and gain valuable first-team experience. The collaboration benefits both clubs, fostering player development and strengthening the relationship between the two communities.
