In Dungeons & Dragons and other fantasy role-playing games, players and dungeon masters often create unique and imaginative names to personalise their gaming experiences. These names can identify the game world's characters, creatures, locations, and magical items. Players can add depth and richness to their characters and world by inventing goblin names.

Goblin names reflect the distinct characteristics of these creatures. Small, agile, and notorious for their deceitful nature. These names tend to be slightly guttural with many sharp sounds. They are often short and easy to pronounce, reflecting the simple nature of goblins.

How do goblins get their name? One way to create names is to draw inspiration from real-world cultures and languages. Players and dungeon masters can also create goblin names by inventing their languages and naming conventions for their game worlds.
