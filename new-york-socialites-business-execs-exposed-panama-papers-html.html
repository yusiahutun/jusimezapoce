New York socialites and business execs exposed in the Panama Papers

 Posted on May 30, 2024  |  12 minutes  |  2520 words  |  Aldo Pusey

An Entourage actress, an art collector and major real-estate investors are among nearly 500 people and companies with New York addresses listed in a Panama Papers database.

Among those named in the searchable database are brothers Joseph, Ralph and Avi Nakash - founders of Jordache Jeans - and other family members.

They are linked to several offshore companies in the British Virgin Islands, The New York Post reported. Those companies include the now defunct Blue Jeans Ltd.

In New York, the Nakash family are major real-estate investors - Joseph is an investor in the New York Wheel currently being built on Staten Island - and also own residential properties and hotels across the country, the New York Post reported.

The International Consortium of ­Investigative Journalists published the database which contains more than 300,000 offshore entities from the Panama Papers and Offshore Leaks investigations.

The data the group has made public is only a fraction of the of the Panama Papers, a compilation of more than 11.5 million leaked files from the Panama-based law firm Mossack Fonseca.

The law firm is one of the world's top creators of hard-to-trace companies, trusts and foundations.

The group has noted that having an offshore account or company is not illegal, noting there are legitimate uses for offshore companies and trusts.

However, they can potentially be used to avoid taxes, hide assets from authorities or conceal wrongdoing.

The group has warned that they are not implying that any person, company or entity included in the database has broken the law or committed any wrongdoing.

Actress Alexis Dziena, who appeared in HBO's Entourage and ABC's Invasion, is listed as an adviser and beneficiary of a trust in the Cook Islands, according to the newspaper.

The 31-year-old's parents, Alexander and Madeline, are beneficiaries of The Theresa Funding Trust, which was established in May 2009.

In 2011, Dziena lost her bid for a restraining order against her parents after claiming they wanted to commit 'murder or violence' in order to gain control of her finances, E Online previously reported.  

Activist and women's health care pioneer, Merle Hoffman, is as a beneficiary of a now defunct trust located in the Cook Islands.

Hoffman, who runs the Choices Women's Medical Center and is publisher and editor-in-chief of On The Issues magazine, was a beneficiary of the Hoffman Family Trust, established in April 2004.

A spokeswoman for Hoffman, who has lived on Park Avenue, told the New York Post that the trust was created around 20 years ago and 'never had any activity'. 

Public records show the 70-year-old still owes more than $800,000 to the Internal Revenue Service and more than $13,000 to the state for what she racked up in federal and state tax liens over the years, according to the Post.

Hoffman's medical center, which was established in 1971 as one of the first ambulatory abortion centers, has since expanded to offering full reproductive health and primary care, according to her website. 

Swiss millionaire Louise Blouin, an art collector and art-magazine publisher, has been named in the Panama Papers as having links with five offshore companies in the British Virgin Islands. 

Blouin, who was born in Montreal and was worth an estimated $425million a decade ago, set up the companies on the advice of her accountants.

She told The Toronto Star: 'I assume everything is in good standing.

'I’m not a lawyer and I’m not an accountant. So anything that was structured (in the) British Virgin Islands was done by Deloitte many, many years ago in the United Kingdom.'

She added that the companies have to do with her real estate transactions, saying she likes to flip properties as a 'hobby'.

Blouin said she was not even aware she had been named until she was contacted by the newspaper.

Last month, her Southampton oceanfront compound with two houses, two pools, and one tennis court was on the market for $145million, but she decided to rent it for $1million after news of her offshore investments came to light, the New York Post reported.

Denise Rich, a wealthy socialite and former wife of pardoned billionaire trader Marc Rich, is linked to two offshore companies, one in the Cook Islands and another in an unknown location.

The 72-year-old is a Grammy-nominated songwriter who wrote songs recorded by Aretha Franklin, Mary J. Blige and Jessica Simpson.

Her ex-husband, commodities trader Marc Rich, fled the United States in 1983 when indicted on charges of tax evasion, fraud, racketeering and illegal trading of oil with Iran. 

They divorced in 1996. Marc Rich received a presidential pardon in 2001 on President Bill Clinton's last day in office. 

Federal prosecutors and Congress investigated the pardon, and in 2002 a House of Representatives committee concluded Denise Rich had swayed the action through donations to the Clinton library and campaign. 

According to records released in 2013 as part of the Offshore Leaks investigation, it revealed she had stored more than $140million in a trust located in the Cook Islands.

This included a Learjet and a 157-ft sailboat, according to the New York Post. 

In 2012, she gave up her US citizenship, a move that tax lawyers at the time said would likely save her tens of millions of dollars or more in U.S. taxes over the long haul.

Kojo Annan, the only son of former UN Secretary-General Kofi Annan, allegedly made a huge profit by purchasing a luxury apartment in London using an offshore company named in the Panama Papers.

According to the leaked Mossack Fonseca documents, the 42-year-old used the Samoan company Sapphire Holding Ltd to buy the property in 2004 for £400,000 ($574,188).

Market data show that it is now worth up to £1,559,293 ($2,238,320).

Annan, who previously rented an apartment in Tribeca, is also linked to two other offshore companies that are now defunct.

This is the latest in a string of allegations about Annan, including that he took advantage of his powerful father to win business contracts, and avoided tax while shipping a green Mercedes 4X4 to Ghana. He has denied any wrongdoing.

Businesswoman and New York realtor Arline Tarte is linked to a company located in the British Virgin Islands.

The 74-year-old, who lives on the Upper East Side, is listed as a shareholder in Challis International Ltd which was established in 1993.

Tarte, who works for real estate firm Brown Harris Stevens, told the Post that she did not know anything about the offshore company and did not recall investing in it.

'I think that's wonderful,' she told the newspaper. 'I'd like to know what I have.' 

John King, who started what has been dubbed the 'Abbey Road of hip-hop', Chung King Studios, serves as the beneficiary for trusts located in the British Virgin Islands and the Cook Islands.

The 63-year-old had stars such as Beyonce, Lady Gaga, Run-DMC and Public Enemy record at the Manhattan studios, which closed last year, according to the Post.

One of the original Wolves of Wall Street Barry Gesser, who was found dead at his home in West Hollywood last December, was tied to two offshore trusts that are now defunct.

Gesser, 52, was listed as protector for the The Martin Funding Trust and as a beneficiary of The Hope Hill Funding Trust. Both trusts were incorporated in 1999.

He served as one of Jordan Belfort's chief advisers and helped film director Martin Scorsese and actor Jonah Hill while the 2013 film about the traders' exploits was being filmed.

His death last December came less than a year after he was caught in bed with socialite Dori Cooperman just two weeks after marrying actress Stacey Alysson. 

The former hedge fund manager pleaded guilty to stock fraud and racketeering charges after Belfort and his associates' penny stock scam was rumbled in 1998.

Gesser was sentenced to three years' probation from 1999 to 2002 for his work with Belfort's company Stratton Oakmont. He also paid out $2.1million. 

Beer entrepreneur Eugene Kashper has been linked to offshore entity CMFOS Ltd, according to the Post.

The 46-year-old, who is a Russian immigrant, bought Pabst Brewing Co in 2014 for a reported $700million.

Kashper, a multimillionaire who paid a whopping $7million for a Soho apartment in 2006, serves as chairman, CEO and chief beer taster at the company.

Computer scientist David Shaw who started a global investment and technology firm worth more than $36billion has been linked to a trust in an unknown offshore location.

Shaw, 65, founder and chairman of D.E. Shaw & Co, is listed as the trust settlor for the Shaw Family Trust I, which was incorporated in 1994.

Dominique Levy, 48, who owns Dominique Levy Gallery is listed as a shareholder of Aldabra International Ltd, incorporated in 2012

He serves as chief scientist of D. E. Shaw Research, LLC and also serves as a Senior Research Fellow at the Center for Computational Biology and Bioinformatics at Columbia University.

Shaw's firm was once described by Fortune magazine as 'the most intriguing and mysterious force on Wall Street', according to the Post.

Entrepreneur Charles Ardai, best known as founder and CEO of Internet service provider Juno, is listed as 'protector' or adviser of the Shaw Family Trust I, the Post reported.

The 46-year-old, who is also founder of Hard Case Crime, is managing director at Shaw's firm. 

The former president and CEO of Ferrari North America, Gianluigi Longinotti-Buitoni, who made his mark selling luxury cars during a recession, is tied to an entity in the British Virgin Islands.

Longinotti-Buitoni, 62, who is a motivational speaker and author is named as a shareholder in the now defunct Goal Multimedia Ltd.

He previously had a state tax lien of $5,379 of which he now owes $32.70, according to the Post.

Manhattan-based psychoanalyst Paola Mieli is listed, along with the Harvard Business School and several others, as a beneficiary of the offshore Claudius Trust located in an unknown offshore location.

The 61-year-old is the founding member and president of Aprés Coup Psychoanalytic Association, a training analyst with Le Cercle Freudien in Paris and teaches at the School of Visual Arts.

James R Mellon II, a banking-family heir, is tied to several offshore trusts, the Post reported.

The 73-year-old Manhattan resident, who is also an author and editor, once bragged to a Swiss newspaper in 2013 that he went around the globe to avoid taxes saying, 'it's all legal, believe me', according to the newspaper.

Mellon, whose great-great-grandfather Thomas Mellon founded the Pittsburgh bank, is known as the family's unofficial historian and wrote a biography on Thomas Mellon.

A London and Madison Avenue art gallery owner has been listed as a shareholder for an offshore entity located in the British Virgin Islands.

Dominique Levy, 48, who owns Dominique Levy Gallery is a shareholder of Aldabra International Ltd, incorporated in 2012. 

A gallery spokeswoman told the Post that the entity is a personal holding company and was 'put in place for estate planning and is fully disclosed in the United States'.

Levy's gallery sells work by famed artists including Jackson Pollock and Gerhard Richter.

Personal investment adviser Charlene Marant was listed as a shareholder of Highland Consultants located in the British Virgin Islands which is now defunct.

The 62-year-old was previously convicted of using elderly clients' money to fund a glamorous lifestyle and pay for her Wall Street office, according to the Post. She was released from prison in 2010. 

Among those named in the papers include Columbia University's Columbia Investment Management LLC which is linked to an entity located in the Cayman Islands.

The LLC, which also manages the university's endowment, is a shareholder in the Haton Polymer & Fibre Corp. 

The university has not given a comment on the investment, the Post reported.

New York University School of Medicine is also tied to offshore entities, located in the British Virgin Islands, including Huntington Investments Holdings and Scraggy Neck Investments Inc, both incorporated in 1999.

A university spokesman told the Post that the accounts were linked to the medical school's former chairman of neurosurgery, Dr Patrick Kelly, and no university funds were part of the transactions.

Kelly had used his personal funds and is no longer employed by the university, according to the Post. 

The Panama Papers dossier linked some of the world's most powerful leaders, including Russian President Vladimir Putin and British Prime Minister David Cameron to offshore companies

The leaked data is the largest ever release of information about offshore companies and the companies' owners, and have revealed how some politicians and celebrities hide money.

The US Justice Department opened a criminal investigation into revelations from the leaked Panama Papers in April, according to an official letter from the office of Manhattan US Attorney Preet Bharara.

The Panama Papers dossier linked some of the world's most powerful leaders, including Russian President Vladimir Putin and British Prime Minister David Cameron to offshore companies.

Iceland's prime minister Sigmundur David Gunnlaugsson, and Spain's industry minister Jose Manuel Soria, were forced to resign when they were tied to shell companies.
