PDC darts player suspended after punching rival during post-match handshake

 Posted on June 11, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  466 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

A professional darts player has been suspended after appearing to punch an opponent.

Adam Smith-Neale, the world number 82, allegedly aimed a punch immediately after losing a match at the Nuneaton Darts Open, a non-PDC amateur tournament. Footage of the alleged assault appeared online and has been widely condemned.

The 30-year-old has now been indefinitely suspended by the sport’s regulatory authority, the Darts Regulation Authority (DRA), pending a disciplinary hearing. A DRA statement read: “On 12th March 2024 Nigel Mawer, the DRA Chairman, took the decision to suspend Adam Smith-Neale from attending or competing in DRA sanctioned events.

“This relates to an allegation that he assaulted another player at the end of his match in an amateur event in Nuneaton played on 9th March 2024. He has the right to appeal the suspension decision and he will face a disciplinary hearing at a date to be arranged.”

Smith-Neale regained his PDC Tour card last year after a five-year absence. He will now miss the upcoming Player Championship 5 and 6 events in Hildesheim, Germany.

A PDC spokesperson said: “The PDC are aware of a video from a non-affiliated event, featuring a Tour Card Holder, which is circulating on social media. The Darts Regulation Authority have been made aware and are investigating this appropriately.”

As a teenager, Smith-Neale showed great potential as he landed a PDC Tour card for the first time. However, he couldn’t hold on to his place at darts’ top table and switched to the BDO, surprisingly winning the World Masters in 2018. That secured him a place at the BDO World Championship the following year but his preparations for the Lakeside showpiece were far from ideal.

He suffered a badly broken leg after, by his own admission, falling over while drunk while on holiday. That resulted in him taking to the Lakeside stage on crutches for his match against Mark McGrath, who took a 3-0 victory.
