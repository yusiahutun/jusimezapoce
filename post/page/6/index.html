ALABAMA (CelebrityAccess) – J.M. “Jimmy” Van Eaton, the pioneering rock ‘n’ roll drummer renowned for his contributions to hits at Sun Records, passed away on Friday (February 9) at his home in Alabama after dealing with some health issues over the past year. His death was confirmed by his daughter Terri Van Eaton Downing. He was 86. A Memphis native, Van Eaton played a crucial role in the early days of rock ‘n’ roll, providing the bluesy beat behind classic tracks like “Whole Lotta Shakin’ Goin’ On” by Jerry Lee Lewis and “Red Hot” by Billy Lee Riley at Sun Records in Memphis, TN.
