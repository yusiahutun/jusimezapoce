The best plus-size brands to shop now

11 Honoré

Think of 11 Honoré as the Net-a-Porter of the plus-size world, an online store offering designer brands that cater to women beyond size 16. Its edit includes fashion heavyweights such as Brandon Maxwell, Carolina Herrera, Prabal Gurung and Marc Jacobs, in addition to its own eponymous line, which focuses on classic, well-made staples with elevated details.

Erdem

British label Erdem is new to the plus-size market, but has pledged a long-term commitment to make the brand inclusive for all. Following customer demand, the designer's ready-to-wear collections are now available for women up to size 22. If romantic, beautifully-made clothes are your bag, then there are few better places to shop.

Reformation

Sustainable brand Reformation made its plus-size line a permanent fixture in 2019, bringing its best-selling cult dresses to a broader market. Whether you're looking for a slinky LBD that will accentuate your curves or a pretty summer dress to dig out year after year, Reformation has you covered.

Marina Rinaldi

Marina Rinaldi is a pioneer of plus-size fashion, having established itself in 1980 as a luxury label that catered directly to curvier women. Today, there is still a focus on soft silhouettes with celebrity fans including Melissa McCarthy, Patricia Arquette and Ashley Graham. Bright colours, prints and embroideries are key at Marina Rinaldi - ideal for women who want to stand out and truly play with fashion.

Ganni

Beloved Scandinavian label Ganni has gradually been expanding its size offering over the past few years. It began with a partnership with 11 Honoré, for which it created an exclusive collection that was offered in sizes UK four to 24. The collection underwent careful research and testing over an eight-month period to ensure flexible fitting possibilities for the customer and, the collaboration was just the beginning of Ganni's journey into more inclusive sizing.

The brand's spring/summer 2022 collection built upon this journey by offering a selection of the ready-to-wear and swimwear in these extended sizes. With future seasons, Ganni has said it is committed to gradually increasing the offering of extended sizes across each collection. See its current edit here.

Coyan

If you're searching for minimalist, fuss-free fashion, then Coyan is a go-to option. The US brand focuses on a curated edit of elegant ethically-sourced silk dresses, each with a versatile appeal and that can be worn casually or more formally. Simple, fluid shapes take centre stage, from floor-length styles to belted V-neck looks in teal. Forget trend-led pieces that date within months - these are luxurious, comfortable designs with an effortless ease.

Kai Collective

Fashion and travel influencer Fisayo Longe established Kai Collective in 2016, aiming to celebrate femininity in all different forms. Directional, bold and luxurious, the capsule offering spans vegan leather dresses, silky shirts in vibrant shades and form-fitting printed dresses and tops covered in an exclusive print by Adebusola Adetona of Grapes Pattern Bank. If you want to make a statement in clothes that truly empower, Kai Collective is a great start.

Universal Standard

Considered the world's most inclusive fashion brand, Universal Standard offers US sizes from 00 to 40 (UK sizes 4 - 44). The label believes that all women should be entitled to style, quality and respect, regardless of their shape, creating quality, contemporary pieces with instant must-have appeal. Its a template that has appealed to the industry, with collaborators including Rodarte and Adidas.

Veronica Beard

US label Veronica Beard launched an extended sizing line in 2019 after its founders realised that the limited size range meant that they weren't reaching as many women as they wanted to. Its collections are characterised by polished, easy-to-wear pieces, from the perfect blazer to well-cut denim and flattering wrap dresses. Look out for its swimwear options too, which this season stars a feminine high-waisted gingham two-piece.

The Hour

The Hour aims to flatter and frame fuller figures, designing seasonless, versatile looks that can be worn day-to-night. Sustainability is a priority and collections are made with upcycled fabrics that would normally be discarded at the mills. Founded by a former doctor based in London, the label takes precision, proportion and careful sculpting seriously, so that pieces never swamp nor restrict.
