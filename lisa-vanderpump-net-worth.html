She’s done quite everything. Lisa Vanderpump’s net worth is super impressive for the empire that she’s built. As an executive producer of her reality show Vanderpump Rules, her net worth is unmatched compared to her Bravo peers.

Vanderump Rules is a reality TV series following the personal and professional lives of the staff at Real Housewives of Beverly Hills alum Lisa Vanderpump’s restaurant SUR Restaurant & Lounge in Los Angeles. The series — which started as a spin-off of The Real Housewives of Beverly Hills in 2013 — was nominated for its first Primetime Emmy Award in the Unstructured Reality Program category in 2023.

The nomination came after Vanderpump Rules Season 10‘s shocking scandal involving cast members Ariana Madix, Tom Sandoval, and Raquel Leviss. The scandal — which has since been nicknamed #Scandoval — came after news broke that Tom and Raquel had a months-long affair while he was still dating and living with Ariana, whom he had been in a relationship with since 2014.

In an interview with Vanity Fair in January 2023, Ariana opened up about returning to Vanderpump Rules Season 11 following #Scandoval. “It’s going to be interesting because it is very much a time capsule. It really is going back in time, and it really is me in a very different place than I’m at right now,” she said. “There’s good days and there’s bad days. There’s ups and there’s downs. There’s definitely going to be those moments—the dancing, having-fun feeling like, I got this. And then there’s going to be moments where maybe I don’t got this. But that’s the journey I was on, and I feel like it’s just my job to let people in on that. So you’re going to see all of it.”

  • Lisa Vanderpump Salary for Vanderpump Rules

    Lisa Vanderpump is an executive producer and main cast member on Vanderpump Rules. She’s starred on the series since Season 1. While Lisa’s current Vanderpump Rules salary isn’t known, The Hollywood Reporter reported in 2020 that Lisa made more than $50,000 per episode at the time as an executive producer and cast member on Vanderpump Rules, along with “lucrative incentives.” The salary would pay Lisa around $1 million for a 20-episode season. The magazine also reported that Lisa’s salary was more than double what Vanderpump Rules‘ main cast members made, which was $25,000 per episode at the time, making her the highest-paid Vanderpump Rules cast member.
