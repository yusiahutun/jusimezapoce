Teen Mom Farrah Abraham 'auctioning Harvard shirt &amp; donating money to mental health foundation' afte

 Posted on March 8, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  485 words  |  Larita Shotwell

TEEN Mom Farrah Abraham will be "auctioning her Harvard shirt and donating the money to a mental health foundation" after she slammed the prestigious school.

Farrah  30, mentioned her auction - which might be running on Monday - in an Instagram story. 

The MTV alum captioned the snapshot: "I think I'm going to auction my Harvard shirt."

The Teen Mom OG star stated in the caption that she would "give the money to a mental health foundation that would help students with therapy."

That caption mentioned that this was "much needed for poor environments at universities."

The "executives furthering their education" also needed support, according to Farrah's caption. 

In early August, the TV star had previously worn a Harvard sweater while boasting about studying for the Law School Admissions Test [LSAT] in a TikTok video.

The former reality star wrote: "P.S. finishing summer semester finals & studying for LSAT. Teen Moms are smart & talented.”

Farrah claimed to be "in a different place” than when she appeared on the MTV show.

THE YELP REVIEW

Recently, the Teen Mom star blasted Harvard as "not a safe nor credible school" in her one-star Yelp review. 

The reality star claimed: "Harvard is pathetic and is educationally abusive to students."

In her review, she accused her teacher Patricia Bellanca and Dean Robert Neugenboren of keeping her "locked out of" zoom for "a course" that she paid for.

She claimed the two "are not responsible professionals" and that their bullying "student tactics" are allegedly "illegal and sexist."

Farrah also claimed she had disputed her tuition after "the teacher claimed [she] had a neurological issue."

The reality star alleged that Harvard's "teachers do not know how to teach online!" 

A spokesperson for Harvard University told TMZ that due to federal student privacy laws, the school will not be commenting on individual student academic matters.

THE SUMMER COURSES

Back in June, a rep for Harvard University exclusively confirmed to The Sun that Farrah was enrolled as a student at the Ivy League. 

The rep told The Sun: “We can confirm that Farrah is registered as of right now in online summer courses.”

Debra, Farrah's mother, previously told The Sun that her daughter was "doing online classes at Harvard to further her entertainment career. It’s not to be an actress. It’s to do production, writing, storytelling, directing, and more. 

“She sent in the receipts to LinkedIn to show she paid for the classes. It’s back up on her LinkedIn. She’s definitely enrolled.”

Debra added that her 30-year-old daughter would not be "on the campus” and would be “doing classes online.”

Back in May, Farrah posted on LinkedIn that she was working towards a Master of Liberal Arts from the Creative Writing and Literature Graduate Program at Harvard.
