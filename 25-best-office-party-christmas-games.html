25 Best Office Party Christmas Games

 Posted on August 10, 2024  |  16 minutes  |  3402 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

If you want to add some merriment to your business holiday party, you could play some games. Put away your search! From icebreakers to group games and holiday trivia questions, we have everything you need to have a successful party.

These games are fun for every size party, from intimate family gatherings to massive office parties. The greatest thing is that you don’t even have to leave your workplace to play them!

Here are the Best Office Party Christmas Games ;

25. Christmas Charades

Charades is a fun game and one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games that can be played by people of all ages, making it ideal for a holiday party at the workplace. Create teams with two or more players. Then, designate one member of each squad as the “charades player.” Everybody else on the squad will hand out hints.

Get ready for the game by making a list of Christmas-related words and phrases to use as prompts on individual pieces of paper. Typical Christmas activities include: “making a gingerbread house,” “wrapping presents,” “eating candy canes,” and “singing songs.” Words for charades may also be derived from well-known Christmas media like as films, music, and TV series.

24. Name That Tune: Christmas Edition

This is a humorous take on the popular game show, “Name That Tune.” Create teams with two or more players. Next, have them choose a “tune master” for their group. The team’s tune master will be in charge of playing Christmas music excerpts for the group to identify. Make a playlist of festive tunes in advance to ensure you have enough options during the game. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

Traditional carols, modern Christmas hits, or any other upbeat music will do. The next step is to devise a system to include musical excerpts into the game. This may be done using a stereo, a laptop, or even your smartphone.

Get the game started by having the first team’s tune master play a short excerpt of a Christmas song. The remainder of their squad has to figure out the song’s title as fast as they can. Whichever group can name the most songs accurately within a certain time limit is declared the winner.

23. Christmas Movie Quotes

This is an exciting new take on the old TV game show Hollywood Squares, and it’s a lot of fun! Create teams with two or more players. Next, choose one member of each group as the “movie quote master.” The team’s movie quote master is in charge of presenting famous movie lines from seasonal favorites for the other players to guess.

Make sure you’re well-prepared for the game by compiling a list of classic holiday films. Then, find quotations from these movies that would be known to most people. Use well-known sayings or come up with your own clever one-liners to make others laugh.

Have the first team’s movie quote master begin their quote whenever you’re ready to begin play. The remainder of their group has to attempt to figure out which film the line is taken from. The winning team is the one that correctly guesses the most lines from the given movies within the allotted time limit.

22. Santa’s Helper Scavenger Hunt

This is a new and exciting take on the traditional holiday scavenger hunt. Create teams with two or more players. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games Then, designate one member of each group as “Santa’s helper.” This elf’s job is to complete the scavenger hunt by locating all of the things on the list.

Plan ahead for the game by compiling a list of Christmas-related goods that may be discovered at the workplace. A candy cane, an ornament of reindeer, a picture of Santa, or a Christmas card are all examples. You may also put in some fun tasks, like shooting a photo with someone wearing a Santa hat or locating an object that begins with each letter of the word “Christmas.”

When you’re ready to play, hand the scavenger hunt list to the first team’s elf and tell them to get to work! The winning team is the one who locates every item on the list in the least period of time.

21. Christmas Carol Pictionary

This is a fun and festive spin on the traditional game of Pictionary. Create teams with two or more players. Then, have them choose one member to serve as the team’s “carol artist.” The carol artist will be responsible for producing graphics that depict popular Christmas melodies. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

Make a playlist of well-known Christmas songs as a warmup for the game. You may utilize old carols, Christmas pop music, or any other form of festive sound. Then, design a means to draw visuals for the game. This may be done using a whiteboard, a chalkboard, or even huge sheets of paper.

Once you’re ready to play, have the first team’s carol artist start sketching their image. The remainder of their team must attempt to guess the name of the Christmas song being represented. The team that predicts the most carols accurately in a given length of time wins the game.

20. Santa’s Workshop

This is a fun and festive take on the traditional board game, “The Game of Life.” Create teams with two or more players. Then, have each team pick one individual to be their “santa.” The santa will be responsible for directing their crew through a number of trials in order to fulfill their presents for Christmas.

To preparation for the game, compile a list of Christmas-themed tasks that teams will need to fulfill. These might be physical obstacles, like wrapping a present or creating a gingerbread house, or cerebral ones, like answering a holiday-themed puzzle or coming up with the ideal Christmas gift for someone. You may even bring in some fun seasonal touches, like having teams wear Santa hats or sing Christmas songs while they work.

Once you’re ready to play, have the first team’s santa start guiding their team through the tasks. The team that completes all of the tasks in the quickest period of time wins the game! one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games

19. Rudolph’s Nose Pick

This is a fun and festive spin on the old game of “Pin the Tail on the Donkey.” Create teams with two or more players. Then, have them choose a team “rudolph.” Each team’s rudolph will be blindfolded and tasked with picking their nose.

Making Rudolf masks and red paper noses in advance of the game is a fun way to get everyone in the spirit. You may even toss in some fun seasonal twists, like having teams wear Santa hats or utilizing Rudolph-themed rewards for the victors. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

The game may begin after the rudolph for the first team begins blindfolded nose plucking. They need the help of the rest of the squad to find the red paper nose. The winning team is the one who removes the most nose hairs in the allotted period.

18. Santa’s Sleigh Ride

This is a fun and festive variation on the traditional game of “Red Light, Green Light.” Create teams with two or more players. Then, choose a single member of each group as the “Santa” for the other groups. The santa must guide his or her squad over a series of obstacles while everyone else is kept in the dark. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

To preparation for the game, compile a list of Christmas-themed tasks that teams will need to fulfill. Wrapping presents or creating a gingerbread house are examples of physical problems; answering a holiday-themed conundrum or thinking of the right present for someone are examples of cerebral challenges. Teams might wear Santa hats and sing Christmas music to put in the spirit as they work.

When everyone is ready, have the first team’s santa begin guiding them through the obstacles. The team that completes all of the tasks in the quickest period of time wins the game!

17. Elf on the Shelf

This new take on the timeless game of “Hide and Seek” is both festive and plenty of fun. Create teams with two or more players. Then, designate one member of each group as the “elf” for that group. The elf will be responsible for hiding someplace in the room while their squad attempts to discover them.

To prepare for the game, compile a list of hiding areas that are off-limits. Some examples of these include the space beneath the desk and the storage space in the closet. You may even bring in some fun seasonal twists, such having teams wear elf hats or use only Christmas-themed terms to communicate. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

Once you’re ready to begin, have the first team’s elf start hiding someplace in the room. The remainder of their crew must attempt to locate them before time runs out. Whoever locates their elf first is declared the winner.

15. 12 Days of Christmas

This is a fun and festive take on the traditional game of “Memory.” Create teams with two or more players. Then, choose a single member of each group as the “Santa” for the other groups. The santa will be accountable for recalling a list of Christmas-themed objects while blindfolded. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

Make a check list of Christmas-related things that each team has to remember before the game. Possible examples include the names of Santa’s reindeer or well-known Christmas songs. Teams might wear Santa hats and sing Christmas music to put in the spirit as they work.

Once you’re ready to play, have the first team’s santa start recalling the list of goods. To win, a team must properly recall the most objects in the least period of time.

14. People Bingo

Playing People Bingo at your holiday party is a fun and interactive approach to let guests mingle and get to know one another. To play, hand out bingo cards to each player that have seasonal symbols. Examples: “Is an expert at making Christmas cookies” or “Has gone skiing.” one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

As individuals interact and converse, they should strive to identify someone who matches each item on their bingo card. They should cross that person off their list as soon as they make contact with them. The first player to get 5 in a row (across, down, or diagonally) wins the game!

We play this every year at our business holiday party, and it’s always a hit.

13. Secret Santa

Want to play a present giving game at the office? When you’re hosting a small company Christmas party, Secret Santa is a great icebreaker game to play. In order to play, everyone should place their name on a slip of paper and into a hat.

Then, have everyone choose a name out of the hat. They will be purchasing a secret Santa present for the individual whose name is drawn. All we ask is that they not go overboard with a price tag that exceeds $10 to the gift’s recipient.

Once everyone knows who their Secret Santa is, the fun of deciding what to buy their coworker can begin. Everyone should bring their gifts to the office on the day of the celebration and place them beneath the tree. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

Have everyone gather in a circle around the tree and take turns opening their presents at the conclusion of the celebration.

12. Santa Hat Game

Looking for some fun, long-lasting, and physically demanding games to play at your office Christmas party? Do you like to play a game? Put on a Santa hat and join in!

A pile of Santa hats makes a wonderful workplace party game! As visitors enter the party location, hand out Santa hats to them. It’s important to choose a single leader. Taking off and putting on the hat will be the duty of the leader. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

Following the leader is the aim of the game. If the leader takes off his or her hat, then you should too. The objective of the game is to remove your hat as soon as the leader does. The last person to wear a hat loses!

Consider establishing a punishment for the last person to wear a hat, such as performing a goofy Christmas song. While playing, the game becomes more difficult while others are talking and serving beverages.

11. Gift Wrap Game

This is one of those hilarious games that is perfect for the corporate holiday party. The purpose of the game is to wrap a gift using just one hand

Divide the group into teams of two or more persons. Distribute a gift wrap roll and a wrapped gift to each group. One member on each team will be the wrapper and the other(s) will be the helper(s) (s). The wrapper must use just one hand to wrap the package. The helper(s) can utilize both hands. The first team to complete wrapping their gifts wins the game! one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

10. Who Knows the Big Boss Best?

All the players will have a good time with this game. This is a game in which players compete to demonstrate their superior knowledge about the boss. To play, have everyone write down their response to each question on a sheet of paper. Then, grab all of the papers and mix them up. The one who gets the most questions correctly wins the game! Here are some example questions:

– What is your boss’s favorite color?

– What is your boss’s favorite food?

– What is your boss’s favorite hobby?

Where did your employer go on their last vacation?

– How many siblings does your boss have?

If you’re lucky enough to work for a boss you get along with, this is one of our favorite Christmas party games to play.

Give each team a tree and let them decorate it way they wish. Then, have everyone vote on which tree they believe is the greatest. The team with the most votes wins the contest!

7. Alphabet Game

The workplace would benefit greatly from this game. The purpose of the game is to see who can locate the most Christmas-related terms that start with each letter of the alphabet. Supply everyone with paper and a writing instrument. They should write down as many Christmas-related terms as they can think of that start with each letter of the alphabet. The winner is determined by who has amassed the most vocabulary at game’s finish.

6. Who’s the Employee?

Do you need team-building Christmas party activities for the office? Consider this a safe bet. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

In this game, everyone attempts to guess whose co-worker is being mentioned. Have everyone in the office take out a piece of paper and jot down the name of a coworker to begin. Finally, gather up all the pieces of paper and shuffle them about. Then, take turns reading aloud the descriptions and seeing who can correctly identify the people being described. Some examples of descriptions are as follows:

This individual always has a coffee in their hand.

This individual serves as the workplace rumor monger.

– This guy is notoriously punctual.

The individual in question consistently puts in extra hours.

This individual abuses sick time.

This is one of our favorite workplace Christmas party games if you want to get your colleagues! But do your best to maintain a pleasant and light tone.

5. How Many Ornaments?

As a part of the game, players take turns attempting to predict the total number of Christmas tree decorations. The game is played by simply noting the total amount of tree decorations. You may then take a vote to determine how many people think there are decorations on the tree. The player whose prediction is the closest to the actual number wins.

4. White Elephant Gift Exchange

White elephant parties are a staple at holiday gatherings. The goal of “white elephant” gift exchanges is to get the most hilarious or useful item. Bring a wrapped present to the party to participate. It’s not important if a gift is brand new or old, but it should be something the recipient wants. After that, the presents are gathered in one spot. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

To begin, have everyone take turns selecting a present from the pile. They may unwrap it and either keep it or exchange it for something else. The aim is to finish the game with the nicest present possible!

3. What’s in the Bag?

This is a fantastic group activity. It’s up to you to figure out what’s hidden in the mystery satchel. You’ll need a lot of little things, which you may put in bags for the game. Assemble unique contents for each bag. The next step is to pass out bags and have everyone take turns choosing one and attempting to guess what’s inside. The winner is the player with the highest percentage of right guesses. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.

2. Two Truths and A Lie

Use this as an excellent conversation starter! The goal is to identify which of two assertions is a fib and which is not. To play, have everyone jot down three self-revelation sentences. The third assertion may be a falsehood, but the other two must be true.

Then, take turns reading your assertions aloud and seeing if your teammates can spot the liar. If you can correctly estimate the most liars, you win the game! Since most people at work are familiar with the rules, it’s always a hit during the holiday party. one of the Best Office Party Christmas Games.
