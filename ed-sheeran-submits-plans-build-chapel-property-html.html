Ed Sheeran submits plans to build a chapel on his property

 Posted on April 8, 2024  |  11 minutes  |  2270 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Ed Sheeran was recently forced to shoot down speculation that he'd already secretly wed his 25-year-old fiancée Cherry Seaborn, after he was spotted wearing a silver ring on his left finger last week.

And now the venue where the singer, 27, and his long-time love will tie the knot may have just been revealed, thanks to the unveiling of a set of plans he's submitted to build a chapel on the grounds of his Suffolk property.

The Castle On The Hill singer, whose wife-to-be was proudly by his side as he scooped four trophies at last week's BRIT Awards, is awaiting planning permission to construct the venue, which will sit just yards from his mansion.

Going to the chapel? Speculation has been sparked that Ed Sheeran plans to wed his fiancée Cherry Seaborn on the grounds of his East Suffolk property, after he submitted plans to construct a private chapel just yards from his mansion

Once constructed, the building - set to be built by architects who have worked on Windsor Castle and Somerset House - will stand at an imposing 32ft tall and will be able to hold an intimate congregation of up to 24 people.

The venue, designed in the form of a small early Suffolk Round Tower Church, would prove particularly perfect for the couple to exchange vows away from prying eyes, as they have been known to fiercely guard their privacy.

In his planning application, lodged in the name of Cherry's architect father Matthew Seaborn, the Shape Of You singer said he wants to build the flint chapel as a place to 'retreat for contemplation and prayer'.

The next few weeks will be something of a waiting game for Cherry and Ed, as the local council won't be expected to announce their decision on the application until April.

Last month, it was revealed Ed has been using his £40million fortune to build his own property empire by snapping up houses in the Suffolk village where he lives. 

He has bought four homes next door to each other to create his own country estate. MailOnline revealed exclusively yesterday how Ed is building an underground ‘man cave’ featuring a music room and cinema beneath the grounds of his main farmhouse.

While Ed has spoken little of his religious views, his 2017 hits Galway Girl and Nancy Mulligan are based on his Wexford-based grandparents’ marriage, which they fought for, despite their Catholic-Protestant divide.

Of his family's religion, Ed said in 2013: 'My dad comes from a very large Irish Catholic family, so he has a very close knit family. He always taught me that family is the most important thing in life, and he told me to choose one thing to work hard at, and make sure you work hard at it. That is why he never pressured me too much in school. I was never a very academic person, but I always showed that I wanted to play music.'

Speaking to Sky News, the chart-topping musician explained that he liked the idea of both of them having a ring to mark their engagement. The star - who is thought to be worth a whopping £40million, thanks to his plethora of hits - clarified: 'It’s an engagement ring, ’cause I feel like — I dunno. I feel like it’s good to both do things.'  

Ed and Cherry are notoriously private about their relationship, but they did reveal they got engaged at the end of last year, sharing the news on Instagram in January. 

His good friend Ellen DeGeneres displayed her signature comedic style, when she tweeted on February 17: 'Happy birthday @EdSheeran! I'm so excited for your wedding. No need to ask... yes! I'll sing.

Ed recently spoke about his relationship with consultant Cherry to pal Taylor Swift in a behind-the-scenes video of the making of their song, End Game. And in particular, the inspiration behind his rap verse in the smash hit.

The Grammy winner revealed in the short video how Taylor, 28, had a helping hand in kick starting his relationship with Cherry.

An aerial view of Ed's four properties nicknamed 'Sheeran-ville' by locals living in his Suffolk village

Talking about their collaborative track, Taylor asked Ed about the thought process behind his lyrics for End Game, beginning: 'I wanna talk about the Fourth of July line because that makes me really happy.'

Responding, he said: 'Well, you see, it’s kind of a play on words — well, not really a play on words. There’s a film with Tom Cruise called Born on the Fourth of July. My relationship started on the Fourth of July.'

Taylor playfully teased: 'At whose house?'

He replied: 'At Taylor’s house. So the story was, I was at Taylor’s party, and then a girl I went to school with who’s pretty cool ended up being in Rhode Island… I was like, "Taylor, can she turn up?" And here we are.'

Taylor then gushed over Cherry, saying: 'Ugh, she is the coolest.' 

A year following Taylor's annual Fourth of July bash, she helped commemorate the pair's anniversary with a rib-tickling pun-filled sign.

Taylor asked: 'Remember the sign that I made for your first anniversary?', which Ed quipped: 'Happy Anniversa-Cherry?' She corrected her pal: 'Happy Ed-iver-Cherry! I’m still excited about it. It took me so long to think of both of your names…'.

In January, Ed announced he got down on bended knee to girlfriend Cherry on social media with a sweet Instagram post of the pair kissing, as he revealed that he proposed last year but chose to keep the news private until recently.
