She certainly has plenty of naked ambition, so it should come as no surprise that Lady Gaga decided to bare in a new advertising campaign for Versace.

However, Gaga is likely to be unimpressed by leaked shots from the campaign that show her pre-photoshop.

And the difference between the washed out model with dark circles and the glistening alabaster beauty from the finished, retouched images is truly astonishing.

Jaw-dropping: A series of pictures reveal Lady Gaga prior to being photoshopped for her Versace campaign

After: Gaga looks incredible in the shot chosen as the final frame for the campaign

A purple handbag protects her modesty as she lazes back on a couch in arguably the most eye-catching of the new images.

And a second image shows her completely topless while sitting on the same settee, with just her blonde locks hanging over her bared breasts.

The 28-year-old pop idol, real name Stefani Germanotta, seemed to have had a great time taking part in the photoshoot.

Less than flawless: The shots show just how much make-up and work Gaga needed to make her campaign-ready

Laid bare: Gaga is unlikely to be happy by the unveiling of the unretouched pictures

Hanging out: Gaga stares sulkily into the camera for another of the frames


As one of the world's most recognisable celebrities, the images are sure to drum up plenty of business for the Italian fashion house.

However her new campaign is in danger of being drowned out due to bad publicity that has engulfed the star.

She was forced to deny reports she wasted 356,000 gallons of water to fill a massive pool for her music video GUY during the worst drought in California history.

Reality bites: The shots show Gaga in a less than flattering light

Posing up: Gaga still looks confident without the help of make-up


Keeping busy: Gaga looks tired in the shots before they are photoshopped to perfection

After photoshop: In the finished images, Gaga looks her usual radiant self

  

A source claimed that in making the short film the Poker Face singer filled the Neptune Pool with heavily chlorinated water at the historic Hearst Castle in Central California, thereby making the potable water unusable to even nurture plants.

However the Poker Face favourite's rep and a spokesperson for the State Of California have dismissed the allegation, telling MailOnline it is a 'made up story'.
