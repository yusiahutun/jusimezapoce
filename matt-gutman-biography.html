Who is Matt Gutman?

Matt Gutman is an American journalist, news reporter, and TV host. Matt Gutman is best known for hosting the ABC wildlife program Sea Rescue.

He is also known for reporting on Nightline, Good Morning America, and many other ABC News programs.

Matt Gutman: Bio, Age, Parents, Siblings, Ethnicity

Matt Gutman was born on December 5, 1977, in  Westfield, New Jersey, United States. As of 2023, he is 45 years old. He is of American nationality and belongs to the Caucasian ethnicity.

He is the son of American parents Paul J. Gutman, father, and Sharon Gutman, mother. He has an elder sister named Rachel Gutman.

They grew up in Westfield, New Jersey. He does not have any brothers.

Education

Matt Gutman attended Williams College and graduated with his bachelor’s degree in 2000 from Newark Academy.

Matt Gutman: Professional Life, Career

After high school graduation, Matt began his career as a Jerusalem-based broadcast journalist.

He worked there for several years and then he joined ABC News in 2008 as a reporter. In 2014, he took over the hosting duties for the ABC wildlife program Sea Rescue.

He also reported for the Nightline, Good Morning America, and many other ABC News programs.

Since 2014, Matt Gutman is serving as the host of the ABC wildlife program Sea Rescue. He is very active on social media and he has more than twenty thousand Twitter followers.

In November 2018, his book The Boys in the Cave, an account of the Tham Luang cave rescue, was released. It has been published in six different languages.
