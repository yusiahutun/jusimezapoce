Chris Pratt says he was 'broken and really struggling' when he met Katherine Schwarzenegger

 Posted on August 8, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  472 words  |  Larita Shotwell

The Mario Bros actor needed 'saving' when he met Maria Shriver's daughter

    Chris Pratt has given an insight into his mental health battles, revealing how his Christian faith and falling in love with wife Katherine Schwarzenegger dragged him out of a dark place.

    The star, 43, told Drew Barrymore he was "really struggling and felt really broken" following his divorce from Anna Faris in 2018. He met Katherine, 33 – who is the eldest daughter of Arnold Schwarzenegger the same year.

    They were married after a whirlwind five-month courtship on June 8, 2019, in Montecito, California, and the adorable couple made their red carpet debut at the premiere of in April 2019.

    On Tuesday’s episode of , Chris, recounted: "There was a moment in my life that I was really struggling and felt really broken.

    "For me, [it was] my own journey finding a higher power and leaning on that and feeling like, 'Please save me,' and feeling saved. And then shortly later, [I met] the woman of my dreams."

    actor Chris, who shares daughters, Lyla Maria, two, and one-year-old Eloise Christina with Katherine – he also has a son, Jack, 10, from his marriage to Anna – met Katherine at a church and quipped how it felt icky to check her out in a place of worship. 

    "I was in the front row of church – which is like, you kind of don't want to be like, 'Whoa, who's that?' at church," he joked. "But I was sneaking some glances and I'm wondering, 'Who is that? Anyway, what am I doing? Come on, I'm broken, help me. But who's that?'"

    He said they didn't start dating right away but hit it off, adding, "God has a fast-forward button, you know? When it’s right – boom – you’re hit. You fall in love, you get married."
