Donatella Versace has joined the ranks of celebrities partaking in the ALS Ice Bucket Challenge.

In a video posted on Wednesday afternoon, the 59-year-old Italian fashion designer is seen wearing a tight white outfit and sitting between two topless young men in Versace chains and clothes.

At the end of the clip, she screams in astonishment as the guys pour ice water over her head from Grecian vases, drenching her bright blonde tresses.

The video was taken in a lush garden, with palm trees and flowers in the background.

Ms Versace - who has a son and a daughter with her husband of 27 years, Paul Beck - then nominates Pharrell, Prince and Spanish film director Pedro Almodovar to take up the challenge.

Fans took to her label's Facebook page to comment on the video. 'Most glamorous ice bucket challenge video ever. QUEEN,' wrote one person.

'Wow, Donatella on this video is so cool and so beautiful. Applause for her,' said another.

The clip has already garnered more than 37,700 'likes' and 18,300 shares since it was posted to Facebook nine hours ago.

Ms Versace is just the latest famous face to join the cause, which has so far raised $94.3million for awareness and research of Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, also known as Lou Gehrig's Disease.

Kate Moss donned a black bikini for her video, which she posted on Saturday. Lily Allen wore a polka dot swimsuit for hers, and Matt Damon went one step further by dousing himself in toilet water.
