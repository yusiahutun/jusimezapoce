Understanding Cardano (ADA) 

History and Inception of Cardano 

Cardano, founded by Charles Hoskinson, is a third-generation blockchain platform that was launched in 2017. It was designed to address some of the limitations of earlier blockchains, particularly in terms of scalability, sustainability, and interoperability. 

Cardano’s Unique Features and Technology 

Ouroboros Proof-of-Stake (PoS) Consensus: Cardano utilizes a PoS consensus mechanism, which offers energy efficiency and scalability advantages over traditional Proof-of-Work (PoW) systems. 

Smart Contract Capabilities: Cardano introduced smart contract functionality with the Alonzo upgrade, allowing developers to build decentralized applications (DApps) on its blockchain. 

Interoperability and Sustainability: Cardano is designed to work seamlessly with other blockchain platforms, promoting interoperability. Additionally, its commitment to sustainability includes environmentally friendly protocols.
