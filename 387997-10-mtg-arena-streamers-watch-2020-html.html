10 best MTG Arena streamers everyone needs to watch in 2020

Magic: The Gathering Arena, commonly known as the MTG Arena, is one of the most played games, both by professional gamers and gaming newbies. Some of the reasons for its popularity are the game’s clean UI and better graphics. While the best MTG Arena streamers also play other genres, the time they spend on this game is an indication that it is worth playing.

Whether you need to know how to play the game or you want to test yourself against the big games in this field, checking out Magic: The Gathering Arena streamers will give you a clear perspective. These gamers have perfected their skills in MTG and they are interesting to watch.

10 best MTG Arena streamers that you need to watch

As gamers keep improving, it is difficult to point out that a specific person is the best overall or will remain at the top a few months down the line. However, as of now, the best Magic: The Gathering Arena streamers based on numbers, playing styles, and audience engagements include:

1. Kacem Alexandre Khilaji (Noxious)

Kacem Alexandre Khilaji, commonly known as Noxious in the gaming community, is a Canadian streamer. While he plays different games, Noxious is a skilled MTG Arena player. Most gaming enthusiasts follow him because of his playing style, hilarious commentaries, and his relatable personality.

Apart from his distinctive playing style, Noxious is exceptionally knowledgeable on MTG Arena theories. During his streams, he explains some concepts which are often ignored by most streamers. Therefore, if you want to understand the MTG Arena’s finer details and tips, this is the first streamer to watch.

Noxious streams mainly on Twitch. He streams as NoxiousLive, and he has 192,137 followers.

2. Amy (Amazonian)

The gaming community is often saturated with male gamers. However, Amy, commonly known as Amazonian, defies this reality by being one of the best MTG Arena streamers. Besides being a skilled gamer, her walkthroughs are more enlightening than most reviews.

3. Gaby Spartz

Gaby Spartz is a career MTG Arena streamer. Her hilarious commentaries and relatable personality have made her one of the most followed personalities in the MTGA community. She also experiments with other genres, such as Expect CCGs and RPGs Adventure.

Besides being one of the best MTG Arena Twitch streamers, she is knowledgeable about the game. When playing, Gaby explains her moves and therefore making her streams interactive. She is also a key contributor to the MTG community, and her views and opinions are highly respected.

4. Sean Plott (Day9tv)

Sean Plott, commonly known as Day9tv, has the biggest Twitch channel dedicated to MT Arena. His streams are a perfect mixture of gaming strategies and comedy. Also, his walkthroughs are detailed for people interested in MTGA.

Watching streams now feels like watching friends play, unlike before. However, Day9tv’s approach does not refute his tag as a professional streamer.

Day9tv streams mainly on YouTube and Twitch. On YouTube, he has over 577,000 subscribers. On the other hand, he has close to 500,000 followers on Twitch.
