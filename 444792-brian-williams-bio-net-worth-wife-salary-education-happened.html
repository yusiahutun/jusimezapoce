Brian Williams' biography

The legendary journalist was born on May 5th, 1959, in Ridgewood, New Jersey, United States. He was born to Dorothy May and Gordon Lewis Williams. His father was an executive vice president of the National Retail Merchants Association, while his mother was an amateur stage actress.

Williams was raised alongside his three elder siblings. His siblings are David, Richards, and Mary Jane Esser.

Education

Brian attended Mater Dei High School, a Roman Catholic high school in the New Monmouth section of Middletown. While in high school, he was the editor for the school newspaper. After completing high school studies, he joined Brookdale Community College.

He later transferred to the Catholic University of America and then George Washington University. Unfortunately, he did not graduate.

Profession

After dropping out of the university, he worked in the White House. He was an intern during the administration of President Jimmy Carter. He later worked as assistant administrator of the political action committee of the National Association of Broadcasters.
