Sky Sports presenter left writhing on the floor after getting hit 'right in the cahunas'

 Posted on August 30, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  311 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

A Sky Sports presenter was left writhing on the floor after being struck in the private parts by a jockey during an interview.

Matt Chapman, who covers horse racing for Sky Sports and ITV, was conducting an interview with former champion jockey Jamie Spencer at Ascot this week when the bizarre incident occurred.

Chapman was just wrapping up the chat with Spencer when suddenly the jockey suddenly darted off screen, with Chapman letting out a cry of pain. As the camera operator backed away, a bent-double Chapman came into view.

Still wincing from the blow to his crown jewels, he pleaded “let’s go for a break” before adding “right in the cahunas, oh God help me” as he sunk to his knees.

The nine second-clip has been shared by Sky’s @AtTheRaces Twitter account and has been viewed tens of thousands of times.

One racing fan cruelly replied “brilliant” while another urged “good lad, next time twice as hard”. Another fan asked: “Best bit of telly in ages, can we form a queue to have a go as well?”

Chapman clearly saw the funny side and later retweeted the clip and wrote: “…tapped up by the pro cush.”

He also hasn’t needed to take a day off work to recover having been on duty at Lingfield Park today (Thursday).

For his part, Spencer has remained fairly quiet, although he did ‘like’ a tweet from fellow jockey Martin Dwyer, who posted “Good shot” with a dart board emoji.
