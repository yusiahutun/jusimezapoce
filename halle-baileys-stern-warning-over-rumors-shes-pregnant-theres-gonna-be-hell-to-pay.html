Halle Bailey's stern warning over rumors she's pregnant - there's 'gonna be hell to pay'

 Posted on August 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  477 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Actress and singer Halle Bailey has hit back at rumors that she is pregnant with her first child after speculation ran rife on social media.

In a new video, Halle Bailey told fans “leave me alone” after comments about her body circulated online. Fans began to think that she and her boyfriend of two years, music star DDG, were set to become parents when the movie star started wearing baggier clothing in public.

Halle Bailey warns, ‘leave me alone’

In a video posted on her Snapchat Story on Saturday (November 18), the Hollywood star blasted people who spoke about her having a “pregnancy nose.” It comes after months of speculation that Halle Bailey is pregnant.

“Listen, if I see one more person say something about my nose one more time, it’s gonna be hell to pay. And you know why?” she continued, “‘Cause I am black. I love my nose.”

She ended the clip with a very clear message: “[What are you] concerned about my nose for? Leave me the hell alone!”

The Disney actress didn’t directly address the possibility of her being pregnant and only showed her face.

Baggier clothes sparked Halle Bailey pregnant rumors

Halle has sported baggier, more casual clothes in the last couple of months and worn flowy, loose dresses when attending red carpet events. However, she told Us Weekly the decision to wear other kinds of clothing was because she wanted to leave behind the mermaid-esque looks after the release of The Little Mermaid.

Despite calling the looks “fire” the movie star said that she wants to “switch it up a little bit and get out of the princessy looks.”

“As I’m getting older and my body is changing, I’m learning who I am. I want to be sexy. I want to feel good.”

She added: “I want to wear clothes that embrace where I am in life.”

DDG seemingly addresses rumors as well

Though his girlfriend hasn’t said anything, the rapper has used his social media to talk about it, though not directly.

After people criticized him and the possibility he had gotten Halle pregnant, with one person calling him a “rich bum” he changed his bio on X, formerly Twitter, to simply read “rich bum” and wrote in a post: “Updated my bio to clear up confusion.”

He later penned in a post that has now been deleted: “The way folks talk about me on here, you’d think I killed somebody … I literally be chillin’,” according to Complex.

He later wrote in a caption on Instagram while sharing pictures from Glamour’s Women of the Year awards in October: “Worried about me so much, they forgot to live their own lives.”
