Madonna watches her eldest son Rocco Ritchie paint a nude model at his art show as she declares hers

 Posted on July 21, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  674 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Madonna was every ich the proud parent as she watched her eldest son Rocco Ritchie paint a nude model at his art show in Milan on Sunday. 

The singer, 65, took to Instagram to share a video to her 19 million followers of clips and photos from the event. 

Rocco, 23, held the joint exhibition with Luigi and Iango, an Italian fashion photography duo who have beautifully captured numerous celebrities over the years - including Madonna.

Budding artist Rocco approached the model, American Lynley Eilers, while smoking a cigarette. They stood on a small stage surrounded by candles and an orchestra.

Lynley then took the cigarette from him and he began to paint her completely naked body, bar a white base coat, with reds, yellows, and blues.

Rocco co-founded his own gallery called Maison Rhed, the name taken from his alias Rhed which he used at the beginning of his career.

The talented artist told Vogue Hong Kong that although neither of his parents are painters, they fostered a love of art in him from a young age.

He said: 'It certainly helped growing up in a house that was passionate about art and culture.

'Neither of my parents are painters, but both are artists in their own right and they taught me to appreciate and respect it. Their shared fascination eventually piqued my interest and since I can remember I've always been surrounded by it.'

Rocco is Madonna's only biological son, conceived with her second husband, Guy Ritchie.

There were previously reports of a strained relationship between Rocco and Madonna, due to Guy and Madonna's custody battle, but all seems to be behind them.
