Gladys Chania Warned Hubby's Lover to Keep off: &quot;She'd Call Him Through Friends&quot;

 Posted on September 27, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  630 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill
  • Politician Gladys Chania was arrested by DCI as the main suspect in the gruesome murder of her husband, George Mwangi
  • According to the sleuths, evidence was found in the deceased's home tying the politician to her hubby's death despite reporting he was missing
  • The detectives shared that the deceased's other woman told them that Chania had warned her to keep off her hubby

Detectives have arrested politician Gladys Chania, accused of killing her husband, engineer George Mwangi.

Mwangi and Chania spent time separately

Mwangi was an astute businessman with interests in real estate and had three children with Chania, the main suspect, with whom they had been married for over two decades.

The DCI shared Mwangi had arrived back in the country on September 13 from Rwanda, where he is a contractor.

However, sources intimated that their over 20-year union seemed to have been undergoing turbulent times as the two would mostly spend their time at separate homes.

According to DCI, Mwangi, a construction engineer fell in love with a woman identified as Lucy Muthoni, who was looking for a rental house sometime in 2021.

He offered her one of his rental houses next to his house and one thing led to another and he would be spotted together at local joints and other social gatherings.

Some of the family members intimated to detectives that the deceased had introduced Lucy to his family as his second wife, and she was well known to the extended family.

On the fateful day of the murder, Lucy had accompanied the deceased to a family function in Kimunyu, where the main suspect was also in attendance. She left the function earlier.

The two, Lucy and Mwangi, agreed to go out on a date the following day, but she got suspicious when he did not call as agreed. She opted to call his two close friends.

"The friends were informed by the main suspect that the deceased had gone for a walk and left his phone behind. Lucy had refrained from calling the deceased when his wife was around after she allegedly gave her a thorough dress down on one occasion," the detectives stated.

Chania, main suspect in murder

Chania, who unsuccessfully vied for the Kiambu woman representative seat in the August General Election, is believed to have masterminded the murder of her husband following her discovery her husband had an affair with another woman.

The report by the Directorate of Criminal Investigations, Chania had sacked their casual labourer who had been with them for four years and brought in a new one days before the murder.

Detectives now believe the politician planned, executed and committed the heinous murder with the help of the new farmhand, now in custody.
